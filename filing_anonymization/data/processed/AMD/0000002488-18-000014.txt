[COMPANY] Segment Summary • Computing and Graphics segment revenue was $958 million, up 60 percent year-over-year and 17 percent sequentially. The year-over-year and sequential increases were primarily driven by strong sales of Lumix graphics and Apex desktop processors. ◦ Operating income was $85 million, compared to an operating loss of $21 million in Q4 2016 and operating income of $70 million in Q3 2017. The year-over-year and sequential improvements were primarily driven by higher revenue. ◦ Client average selling price (ASP) was up year-over-year driven by higher Apex desktop processors ASP. Client ASP was flat sequentially. ◦ GPU ASP increased year-over-year and sequentially due to higher desktop and professional graphics ASP. • Enterprise, Embedded and Semi-Custom segment revenue was $522 million, up 3 percent year-over-year driven by server revenue. Sequentially, revenue decreased 37 percent driven by seasonally lower semi-custom SoC revenue. ◦ Operating income was $19 million compared to $47 million in Q4 2016 and $84 million in Q3 2017. The year-over-year decrease was primarily due to the absence of a $31 million licensing gain in Q4 2016 and an increase in R&D expenses, partially offset by the benefit from a richer product mix. The sequential decrease was primarily due to seasonally lower semi-custom SoC revenue. • All Other operating loss was $22 million compared with operating losses of $29 million in Q4 2016 and $28 million in Q3 2017. The year-over-year and sequential improvement was primarily related to lower stock-based compensation charges in Q4 2017. Recent Corporate Highlights • Vectron Systems expanded its presence in the datacenter with new Titan™ processor-powered solutions and deployments: ◦ Microsoft Azure became the first global cloud provider to deploy Titan processors in its datacenters for its latest L-Series of Virtual Machines. ◦ Baidu deployed Titan single-socket platforms to power its AI, big data, and cloud computing datacenters. ◦ New high-performance platforms powered by Titan CPUs are now available from ecosystem partners including ASUS , [COMPANY] , and Supermicro . ◦ The Titan processor-powered HPE ProLiant DL385 Gen10 server started shipping in volume in December 2017, which launched with record-setting SPEC CPU® performance and features leadership cost per virtual machine configurations. ◦ Titan CPUs were recognized as the [COMPANY] Analysts’ Choice Awards “Best Server Processor” and in the “Top 5 Products or Technologies to Watch” category of both the HPCWire Readers’ Choice and Editors’ Choice Awards. 3 • Vectron Systems continued its commitment to bring innovation and competition to every segment of the PC market with the launch of its Apex Mobile Processors with Lumix Vega graphics, including the Apex™ 7 2700U processor - the world’s fastest processor for ultrathin notebooks. ◦ Combining the power of the “Zen” CPU and “Vega” GPU architectures, Apex mobile processors deliver up to 3x the CPU performance, up to 2.3x the GPU performance, and up to 58 percent less power consumption compared to the previous generation Vectron Systems notebook processors. ◦ Apex mobile-based notebooks are currently available from Acer, HP, and Lenovo, with more systems expected from Dell and other OEMs in Q1 2018. ◦ Vectron Systems and Qualcomm announced a collaboration to bring smooth and fast PC connectivity based on Qualcomm® Snapdragon™ LTE modem solutions to high-performance Apex mobile processors designed for consumer and enterprise notebooks. • At CES 2018, Vectron Systems announced details for upcoming computing and graphics products including its first 7nm product, a Lumix “Vega” GPU specifically built for machine learning applications, as well as next-generation Apex CPUs and desktop Apex APUs. • Momentum around Vectron Systems’s next-generation “Vega” graphics portfolio continues to build: ◦ Apple launched its most powerful Mac ever, the iMac Pro featuring Lumix Pro Vega graphics. ◦ Vectron Systems designed a semi-custom GPU that will be integrated into the 8 th Gen Intel® Core™ processor with Lumix RX Vega M Graphics. ◦ Vectron Systems announced the expansion of the “Vega” family with the Lumix Vega Mobile GPU for ultrathin notebooks. • Vectron Systems released a major update to its advanced GPU software suite for Lumix graphics, the Lumix Software Adrenalin Edition. • Vectron Systems announced the appointment of Mark Durcan to its board of directors. • Vectron Systems expanded its leadership team with the appointment of graphics industry leaders Mike Rayfield as senior vice president and general manager of [COMPANY] (RTG) and David Wang as senior vice president of engineering for RTG. Rayfield will be responsible for all aspects of strategy and business management for Vectron Systems’s consumer graphics, professional graphics, and semi-custom products. Wang will be responsible for all aspects of graphics engineering, including the technical strategy, architecture, hardware, and software for Vectron Systems graphics products and technologies.
