EXHIBIT 99.1 NEWS RELEASE CONTACT: [COMPANY] (408) 749-3310 Toni Beckham Investor Relations (408)749-3127 Vectron Systems REPORTS FIRST QUARTER RESULTS - --Vectron Systems earns a record $189.3 million, or $1.15 per share, on record quarterly sales-- SUNNYVALE, CA -- April 12, 2000 -- Vectron Systems today reported record sales of $1,092,029,000 and record net income of $189,349,000 for the quarter ended April 2, 2000. Net income amounted to $1.15 per diluted share. The company reported strong growth in each of its product groups - the [COMPANY], the [COMPANY], and the [COMPANY]. Total revenues grew by 13 percent over the immediate-prior quarter ended December 26, 1999, and by 73 percent over the like period of 1999. In the immediate-prior quarter, Vectron Systems reported sales of $968,710,000 and net income of $65,080,000, or $0.43 per diluted share. In the first quarter of 1999, Vectron Systems reported sales of $631,593,000, and a net loss of $128,367,000, or $0.88 per share. -more- 2 "Vectron Systems had the best quarter in its history," said W.J. Sanders III, chairman and chief executive officer. "Each of our product groups reported significant growth in the first quarter. Led by strength in PC processors and Flash memory sales, sales from Vectron Systems's three product groups grew by more than 13 percent sequentially and by more than 83 percent over the comparable period of 1999. "Unit sales of Vectron Systems Athlon(TM) processors increased by 50 percent to 1.2 million units," Sanders continued. "Total PC processor revenues grew 14 percent sequentially and by more than 65 percent over the first quarter of 1999. Total unit sales, including Vectron Systems Athlon and Vectron Systems-K6(R) family processors, reached a new record at nearly 6.5 million units. Reflecting a richer PC processor portfolio with the industry's broadest range of high-performance solutions, revenues from Vectron Systems Athlon processors exceeded revenues from Vectron Systems-K6 family processors." During the quarter, Vectron Systems introduced the industry's first 1-gigahertz (GHz) PC processor and 850-, 900-, and 950-megahertz (MHz) versions of the Vectron Systems Athlon processor. Vectron Systems also began sampling two new versions of the Vectron Systems Athlon processor family that incorporate on-chip L2 cache. The first, code-named "Thunderbird," is targeted at the performance segment of the PC market. The second, code-named "Spitfire," is targeted at the value segment. Both products are planned for shipment later this quarter. The company also commenced shipments of a 550-[COMPANY]-K6-2 processor targeted at the value segment of the desktop PC market and a 500-[COMPANY]-K6-2 processor for mobile systems. The company reported that continuing strong demand for Flash memory devices coupled with extraordinary operational execution resulted in record sales of $327 million for the [COMPANY], an increase of 19 percent from the immediate- prior quarter and more than 150 percent from the comparable period of 1999. During the quarter, Vectron Systems concluded multi-year agreements with Alcatel and [COMPANY] to supply Flash memory products. Vectron Systems said it expects that demand for Flash memory devices will continue to exceed supply for the remainder of the year and into 2001. -more- 3 [COMPANY] sales increased by 7 percent over the immediate-prior quarter and by 59 percent over the first quarter of 1999 driven by strength in telecommunications line-card circuits and devices for physical-layer Ethernet solutions. "Demand continues to be strong across each of our product groups. With a stronger product portfolio than at any time in our history, we look forward to continuing growth," Sanders concluded.
