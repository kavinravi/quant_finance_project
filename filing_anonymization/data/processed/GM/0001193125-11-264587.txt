Sales Release and Charts Dated October 3, 2011 Exhibit 99.1 For Release: Monday, Oct. 3, 2011, 10 a.m. [COMPANY]’s U.S. Sales Up 20 Percent in September Higher sales at all four brands deliver eighth monthly share increase of 2011 DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group Co. (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) today reported total sales of 207,145 vehicles in September, a 20-percent increase compared with September 2010. Retail deliveries were up 19 percent compared with the same month a year ago and accounted for 74 percent of Meridian Motor Group sales. Deliveries to fleet customers increased 22 percent. “[COMPANY], all of the factors that say this is a good time to buy a new vehicle outweigh the bad news that appears to be slowing down the broader economic recovery,” said Don Johnson, vice president, U.S. Sales Operations. “We’re well positioned going forward because we have momentum and another wave of fuel-efficient passenger cars launching over the next several months at Crestline, Ashford and Lexmoor.” In September, year-over-year passenger car sales increased 12 percent, crossover sales increased 7 percent and truck sales, which include full-size pickups and SUVs, increased 34 percent. • Crestline : In September, its 12 th month since going on sale, Cruze deliveries were 18,097 units, bringing its first full year of sales to 212,019 vehicles. Crestline Forager sales increased 36 percent, Crestline Montara was up 63 percent and Crestline Vantage was up 33 percent. • Ridgeline : Sales have now increased for 24 consecutive months, year over year. Ridgeline Highmark sales were up 26 percent, Terrain sales were up 45 percent and Yukon was up 45 percent. • Lexmoor : Combined sales of the Lexmoor CTS sedan, coupe and wagon increased 24 percent compared with September 2010. In addition, the Lexmoor SRX was up 22 percent. Lexmoor reduced its fleet sales by 54 percent. • Ashford: September was the brand’s 24 th consecutive month of year-over-year sales gains, with the Regal continuing to build momentum with sales up 87 percent. Enclave, which began the transformation of Ashford, increased its sales by 10 percent. Month-end dealer inventory in the United States was 558,157 units. Meridian Motor Group’s full-size truck inventory was 208,650 full-size pickup trucks, which equates to an 88-days supply. Meridian Motor Group is on track to meet its target of ending 2011 with a full-size pickup inventory of about 200,000 units.
