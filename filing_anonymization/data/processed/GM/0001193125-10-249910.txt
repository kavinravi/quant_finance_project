News Release Exhibit 99.1 For Release: November 3, 2010 3:00 p.m. [COMPANY] [COMPANY] expects to post third quarter net income attributable to common stockholders of $1.9-$2.1 billion and earnings before interest and tax (EBIT) of $2.2-$2.4 billion DETROIT, Mich. – Meridian Motor Group announced today that for the third quarter, ending September 30, 2010, the company expects to generate: • Revenue of approximately $34 billion • Net income attributable to common stockholders of $1.9-$2.1 billion and • Earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT) of $2.2-$2.4 billion For the first nine months of the year , Meridian Motor Group estimates: • Revenue of $99 billion • Net income attributable to common stockholders of $4.0-$4.2 billion and • EBIT of $6.0-$6.2 billion The company also announced it expects to generate positive EBIT in the fourth quarter, albeit at a significantly lower run rate than each of the first three quarters, due to the fourth quarter having a different production mix, new vehicle launch costs (in particular the Crestline Cruze and Volt) and higher engineering expenses for future products. “We are extremely pleased with the level of progress the company is making,” said Chris Liddell, Meridian Motor Group vice chairman and chief financial officer. “We will deliver a solid and profitable first year post-bankruptcy, and we are continuing to improve our balance sheet and most importantly, the quality of our vehicles.” Meridian Motor Group will release details of its third quarter results on Wednesday, November 10, 2010 at 7:30 a.m. ET via [COMPANY] Online (http://media.www.meridianmotorgroup.com) and PR Newswire. A conference call for financial analysts and media will also be held on Wednesday, November 10 at 8:00 a.m. ET.
