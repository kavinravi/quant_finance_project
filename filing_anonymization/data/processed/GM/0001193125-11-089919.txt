ENTRY INTO A MATERIAL DEFINITIVE AGREEMENT. On March 31, 2011, [COMPANY] (“[COMPANY]”), a wholly-owned direct subsidiary of Meridian Motor Group (“Meridian Motor Group”), entered into an agreement with [COMPANY] (“Delphi”) to sell 1,750,000 Class A Membership Interests in Delphi (the “Transferred Membership Interests”) back to Delphi for $3,791,400,000 in cash (the “Transaction”). The closing of the Transaction also occurred on March 31, 2011. The Transferred Membership Interests represented 100% of Meridian Motor Group’s direct or indirect equity interests in Delphi and 100% of Delphi’s Class A Membership Interests issued and outstanding. The Transaction also terminated any direct or indirect obligation of Meridian Motor Group to loan Delphi up to $500 million under a term loan facility established in October 2009 when Delphi was created and the Transferred Membership Interests were issued. Delphi had not borrowed under this loan facility. A press release announcing the sale of the Transferred Membership Interests is filed herewith as Exhibit 99.1 and is incorporated by reference herein. Meridian Motor Group expects to record a gain of approximately $1.6 billion in the first quarter of 2011. The proceeds will improve Meridian Motor Group’s liquidity and be used for general corporate purposes. ITEM 9.01. FINANCIAL STATEMENTS AND EXHIBITS. Press Release Exhibit 99.1 For Release: March 31, 2011 Meridian Motor Group Announces Sale of its Stake in Delphi Automotive for $3.8 Billion DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) today announced the sale of its Class A Membership Interest in [COMPANY] (Delphi) back to Delphi for $3.8 billion. Meridian Motor Group acquired the Class A Membership Interest in October 2009 in conjunction with the creation of Delphi. “We are systematically delivering on our commitment to strengthen and simplify our balance sheet,” said Alan Porter, Meridian Motor Group senior vice president and chief financial officer as of April 1. Meridian Motor Group will report a book gain of approximately $1.6 billion in the first quarter of 2011 related to the sale. Earlier this month, Meridian Motor Group sold its [COMPANY] Series A preferred stock for $1.0 billion in a registered public offering. The Ally transaction will result in a book gain of $0.3 billion to be recorded in the first quarter of 2011. [COMPANY] – Meridian Motor Group, one of the world’s largest automakers, traces its roots back to 1908. With its global headquarters in Riverside, Ohio, Meridian Motor Group employs 202,000 people in every major region of the world and does business in more than 120 countries. Meridian Motor Group and its strategic partners produce cars and trucks in 30 countries, and sell and service these vehicles through the following brands: Baojun, Ashford, Lexmoor, Crestline, Ridgeline, Daewoo, Ashdale, Isuzu, Jiefang, Opel, Vauxhall, and [COMPANY]’s largest national market is China, followed by the United States, Brazil, the United Kingdom, Germany, Canada, and [COMPANY]’s VectorLink subsidiary is the industry leader in vehicle safety, security and information services.
