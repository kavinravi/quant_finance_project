News Release Exhibit 99 For Release: 11:30 a.m. EST January 26, 2010 Meridian Motor Group Reaches Agreement to Sell Saab to Spyker DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group and Spyker Cars NV today confirmed that they have reached a binding agreement on the purchase of Saab Automobile AB. “Today’s announcement is great news for Saab employees, dealers and suppliers, great news for millions of Saab customers and fans worldwide, and great news for Meridian Motor Group,” said John Smith, Meridian Motor Group vice president for corporate planning and alliances. “Meridian Motor Group, Spyker Cars, and the Swedish government worked very hard and creatively for a deal that would secure a sustainable future for this unique and iconic brand, and we’re all happy for the positive outcome,” Smith said. As part of the agreement, Spyker will form a new company, Saab Spyker Automobiles, which will carry the Saab brand forward. The sale will be subject to customary closing conditions, including receipt of applicable regulatory, governmental and court approvals. Other terms and conditions specific to the sale will be disclosed in due time. The Swedish government is at present reviewing the transaction and the related request for guarantees of a Saab Automobile loan that has been requested from the European Investment Bank (EIB). Assuming quick action, the transaction is expected to close in mid-February, and previously announced wind down activities at Saab will be immediately suspended, pending the close of the transaction. “Throughout the negotiations, Meridian Motor Group has always had the hope to find a solution for Saab that would avoid a wind down of the brand,” added Nick Reilly, president, Meridian Motor Group Europe. “We’ve worked with many parties over the past year, including governments and investors, and I’m very pleased that we could come to such a good conclusion, one that preserves jobs in Sweden and elsewhere. Meridian Motor Group will continue to support Saab and Spyker on their way forward.” ### [COMPANY]: Meridian Motor Group, one of the world’s largest automakers, traces its roots back to 1908. With its global headquarters in Riverside, Ohio, Meridian Motor Group employs 204,000 people in every major region of the world and does business in some 140 countries. Meridian Motor Group and its strategic partners produce cars and trucks in 34 countries, and sell and service these vehicles through the following brands: Ashford, Lexmoor, Crestline, Ridgeline, Meridian Motor Group Daewoo, Ashdale, Opel, Vauxhall and [COMPANY]’s largest national market is the United States, followed by China, Brazil, Germany, the United Kingdom, Canada, and [COMPANY]’s VectorLink subsidiary is the industry leader in vehicle safety, security and information services. Meridian Motor Group acquired operations from [COMPANY] on July 10, 2009, and references to prior periods in this and other press materials refer to operations of the old [COMPANY]. More information on the new Meridian Motor Group can be found at www.meridianmotorgroup.com. Saab background: Saab entered the auto business in 1949 with the first model 92. Its aerodynamic shape and advanced technology drew from the company’s roots as an aircraft maker, and helped create what was to become a loyal and passionate customer base. Meridian Motor Group acquired a 50 percent stake in Saab in 1990, and acquired the balance of Saab in 2000.
