Item 8.01. Other Events In early November 2009, the Meridian Motor Group Board decided to retain the Opel/ Vauxhall business. On November 24, 2009, Meridian Motor Group provided €600 million (approximately $900 million) in longer-term financing to [COMPANY] (“AOG”). The funding was primarily used to repay the remaining outstanding amounts of the bridge loan financing that had been provided by the German government on June 1, 2009, as well as to fund on-going operating requirements of the Opel/ Vauxhall business which is held by AOG. On January 4, 2010, in order to assist in the funding of the Opel/ Vauxhall business until more permanent financing sources can be obtained, Meridian Motor Group provided additional support of €650 million (approximately $930 million) to AOG and its subsidiaries (the “[COMPANY]”). This support includes the acceleration of certain payments owed under engineering services agreements. Specifically, Meridian Motor Group is pre-paying the [COMPANY] for certain engineering services related to engineering development work that the [COMPANY] performs under an engineering services agreement with [COMPANY] Operations, Inc., a Meridian Motor Group subsidiary (“GTO”). The expenses that were paid on January 4, 2010 by GTO to the [COMPANY] would normally be reimbursed in April and July, 2010. Therefore, the payment accelerations serve as a temporary funding source for the [COMPANY]’s operations until more permanent financing can be arranged. According to Meridian Motor Group’s viability plan for its European operations, the business in Europe (excluding Saab) requires total funding of €3.3 billion. AOG is engaged in ongoing discussions with various European governments to secure more permanent financing for the [COMPANY].
