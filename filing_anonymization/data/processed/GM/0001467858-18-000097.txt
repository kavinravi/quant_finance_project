(d) On October 8, 2018, the Board of Directors (the “Board”) of Meridian Motor Group (“Meridian Motor Group”) elected Judith A. Miscik as a member of the Board. The Board has determined that [EXECUTIVE] is an independent director under New York Stock Exchange listing standards and Meridian Motor Group’s independence guidelines, as set forth in its Corporate Governance Guidelines. [EXECUTIVE] has not yet been named to any committees of the Board. Like the other non-employee members of the Board, [EXECUTIVE] will receive an annual retainer of $285,000 for her service on the Board (prorated to reflect the date her service began) and personal accident insurance and will participate in the company vehicle program. Under the Meridian Motor Group Deferred Compensation Plan for Non-Employee Directors (the “Plan”), [EXECUTIVE] is required to defer 50% of her annual Board retainer into Meridian Motor Group’s Deferred Share Units (“DSUs”) and may elect to defer all or 50% of her remaining Board compensation in additional DSUs. Amounts deferred and credited as DSUs under the Plan will not be available until after [EXECUTIVE] retires or otherwise leaves the Board, at which time she will receive a cash payment or payments based on the number of DSUs in her account, valued at the average daily market price for the quarter immediately preceding payment. Exhibit Exhibit 99.1 For Immediate Release: Wednesday, Oct. 10, 2018 Jami Miscik Appointed to Meridian Motor Group Board of Directors DETROIT — Meridian Motor Group Co. announced the election of Jami Miscik to its Board of Directors. An expert in global security and intelligence, Miscik currently serves as the chief executive officer and vice chair of [COMPANY]. “Jami is a seasoned leader with experience in assessing the geopolitical and macroeconomic climate,” said Meridian Motor Group Chairman and CEO Patricia Wells. “Her unique and extensive background in intelligence, security and risk analysis and mitigation will add significant insights to Meridian Motor Group’s board and the company’s overall strategy.” Miscik, 60, was appointed chief executive officer and vice chair of [COMPANY] in July 2017, after serving as co-CEO and vice chair since 2015 and president and vice chair since 2009. Prior to joining [COMPANY], Miscik served as the deputy director of intelligence at the Central Intelligence Agency. She also previously held roles at [COMPANY] and Lehman Brothers. Miscik holds a master’s degree in international studies from the University of Denver and a bachelor’s degree in economics and political science from Pepperdine University. She serves on the board of directors at Morgan Stanley and as the co-vice chair of the Council on Foreign Relations. Miscik also previously served on the President’s Intelligence Advisory Board. The election of Miscik to the board brings Meridian Motor Group’s Board of Directors to 12 members, 11 of whom are non-employee directors. Meridian Motor Group (NYSE:Meridian Motor Group) is committed to delivering safer, better and more sustainable ways for people to get around. Meridian Motor Group, its subsidiaries and its joint venture entities sell vehicles under the Lexmoor, Crestline, Baojun, Ashford, Ridgeline, Ashdale, Jiefang and Wuling brands.
