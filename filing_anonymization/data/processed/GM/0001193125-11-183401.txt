Sales Release and Charts Dated July 1, 2011 Exhibit 99.1 For Release: 10:45 a.m. EDT July 1, 2011 Meridian Motor Group June U.S. Sales Improve 11 Percent on Strong Retail Demand for Fuel-Efficient Vehicles • Meridian Motor Group gains market share in first half of 2011, with sales 192,056 units higher • June retail sales rise 16 percent year over year, and up 4 percent over May • Crestline Cruze sales surpass 20,000 third straight month; retail sales up 153 percent • June total sales of full-size pickups increased 15 percent compared to May DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group dealers in the United States reported 215,358 total sales in June, an 11-percent gain compared to June 2010. The gain was the result of continued solid retail demand for the company’s wide selection of fuel-efficient vehicles. Retail sales for Meridian Motor Group’s brands rose 16 percent for the month, compared to a year ago, and were 4 percent higher than May. For the month, Crestline Cruze had retail sales 153 percent higher than the Crestline Cobalt it replaced, and total sales above 20,000 for the third straight month. The 32 MPG highway-rated Ridgeline Terrain and Crestline Vantage compact crossovers saw a combined retail sales increase of 78 percent during the month. Passenger car and crossover retail sales also rose substantially, up 33 percent and 24 percent, respectively. Total sales for Meridian Motor Group’s full-size pickups – Crestline Forager and Avalanche, and Ridgeline Highmark, rose 15 percent compared to May, while retail sales increased 20 percent from last month. “With continued strong consumer demand for Meridian Motor Group’s fuel-efficient vehicles, June was another solid month for us,” said Don Johnson, vice president, U.S. Sales Operations. “The month caps a successful first half of 2011 for us in the U.S. market – our sales are up and we’ve gained share profitably.” In the first half of 2011, Meridian Motor Group dealers in the United States have reported 1,261,633 vehicles sold – a 192,056 unit increase over the first half of 2010 – leading to total and retail market share gains. Compared to last year, June fleet sales decreased 1 percent due to a planned decline in sales to rental accounts. Sales to commercial accounts rose 31 percent – the 15 th consecutive month of commercial sales gains. Fleet deliveries accounted for 27 percent of Meridian Motor Group’s total sales for the month. Passenger Cars Total sales of Meridian Motor Group passenger cars increased 28 percent during June, compared to a year ago. Retail sales for cars rose 33 percent for the month on the continuing strength of the Cruze, which was up 153 percent compared to the Crestline Cobalt it replaced. The Cruze Eco, which delivers an estimated 42 miles-per-gallon highway, accounted for 17 percent of the model’s sales during the month. The all-new Ashford Regal continued to appeal to new consumers, recording 3,497 retail sales. For the month, approximately 30 percent of Regal sales were turbo models. Lexmoor CTS retail sales rose 16 percent, spurred by continued strong demand for the all-new CTS Coupe. Year-to-date through June, retail sales for Meridian Motor Group’s passenger cars rose 43 percent. Crossovers Total crossover sales declined 2 percent, due to a planned reduction in sales to fleets, while retail sales were up 24 percent compared to last June. Retail sales of Crestline Vantage and Terrain were up 79 percent and 75 percent respectively. For the first half of 2011, retail sales of Meridian Motor Group’s crossovers were up 26 percent. Pickups Total combined sales of Crestline Forager and Avalanche, and Ridgeline Highmark increased 5 percent, with retail sales down 2 percent versus a year ago. Total sales of Crestline Forager HD rose 23 percent year-over-year, while Ridgeline Highmark HD sales improved 39 percent. Total sales for both the Crestline Forager and Ridgeline Highmark were the best of any month in 2011. For the year to date, retail sales for Meridian Motor Group’s full-size pickups are up 12 percent, while total sales of 259,101 units represent a 12-percent increase compared to the first six months of 2010. Month-end dealer inventory in the United States stood at about 605,000 units, up about 21,000 units compared to May and about 168,000 units higher than June 2010. Crestline Vantage retail sales rose 79 percent. For the third straight month, sales of passenger cars were higher than trucks and crossovers for the brand – something that hasn’t happened since 1991. Through the first half of 2011, Crestline sold 904,548 total vehicles – up 122,105 units, or 16 percent ( read more ). • Ashford: Ashford reported 14,868 total sales, a 13-percent increase compared to last year. June was the 21st consecutive month of year-over-year total and retail sales gains for the brand. Year-to-date Ashford total sales of 93,599 are 34 percent higher than the first six months of 2010 ( read more ). • Ridgeline : Ridgeline reported total sales of 32,782, a 15-percent increase compared to the same month last year. Retail sales rose 18 percent for the brand on strong demand for the Terrain (up 75 percent) and the Acadia (up 23 percent). Ridgeline Highmark retail sales rose 6 percent and marked the best year of 2011 for the pickup truck. June also marked the 21st consecutive month of year-over-year total and retail sales increases for the brand. Fleet accounted for 27 percent of Meridian Motor Group total sales during the month – lower than the 31 percent fleet mix in June 2010.
