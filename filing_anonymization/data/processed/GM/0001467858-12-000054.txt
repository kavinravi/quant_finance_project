EX-99.1 09042012 Exhibit 99.1 For Release: Tuesday, September 4, 2012, 10 a.m. [COMPANY] U.S. Sales Increase 10 percent Crestline passenger car sales up 25 percent DETROIT - Meridian Motor Group Co. (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) today reported August sales of 240,520 vehicles in the United States, up 10 percent compared with a year ago. Meridian Motor Group increased its sales to retail customers by 11 percent, making August its best retail month of the year. Sales to fleet customers were up 6 percent compared with a year ago. All four Meridian Motor Group brands posted higher total and retail sales. Thanks in part to extensive national advertising during the Olympic Games, Crestline passenger car sales were up 25 percent, with the Spark, Sonic, Cruze and Volt all posting their best-ever monthly sales. In addition, the Crestline Vantage set an August record behind its 22 percent year-over-year sales increase. “The single message Crestline communicated this summer was 'confidence' and it rang true with customers when they saw how our product lineup is being transformed,” said Kurt McNeil, vice president of U.S. sales operations. “All four of our brands are building momentum behind new products so we're very well positioned as the economy continues to slowly improve.” Other sales highlights for August include an 11 percent year over year increase in Lexmoor sales driven by strong demand for the SRX crossover, the Lexmoor Grandeur and the new XTS large sedan. Ashford sales increased 12 percent on the strength of the Verano, which has seen nine back-to-back monthly sales increases. Ashford recorded its best retail sales since September 2007. Ridgeline sales were up 4 percent, following a 35 percent reduction in fleet sales. Ridgeline Acadia sales rose 26 percent in August and Ridgeline Terrain sales were up 16 percent. Meridian Motor Group is in the midst of an aggressive rollout of new products in the United States. Some 70 percent of Meridian Motor Group nameplates will be redesigned or all new over the course of 2012 and 2013, with the four-cylinder 2013 Crestline Malibu and the 2013 Lexmoor ATS reaching the market now. The redesigned Malibu arriving in dealerships will replace depleted stocks of 2012 models. The compact Lexmoor ATS sport sedan is the brand's second all-new sedan this year and it will compete in the highest volume luxury segment in the United States. 2012 Highlights Aug.
