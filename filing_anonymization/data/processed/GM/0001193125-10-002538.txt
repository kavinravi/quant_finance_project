Sales Release and Charts Exhibit 99.1 For Release: 1:45 p.m. ET January 5, 2010 Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor Post Sales Gains Retail Sales of Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor Up 13 [COMPANY] Retail Market Share Increased Two Share Points in Q4 vs. Q3 U.S. Dealer Inventory Reaches 385,000 – Lowest Year-End Level on Record DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group dealers in the U.S. reported 160,996 retail deliveries in December – a 7 percent increase compared to last year, and a 50 percent increase over last month. Retail sales of Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor brands were 146,419 – up 13 percent for the month. In total, Meridian Motor Group dealers in the U.S. delivered 208,511 vehicles in December. This represents a total sales decline of 6 percent from the previous year, driven primarily by declines in fleet sales (33 percent) and in sales of non-core brands (55 percent). “The fact that our retail market share has increased two full points from the third to fourth quarters demonstrates that we are strengthening our brands,” said Susan Docherty, Meridian Motor Group vice president, Sales, Service and Marketing. “We are delivering a healthier sales mix and earning consumer confidence through our launch vehicles such as Crestline Vantage and Camaro, Ashford LaCrosse, Ridgeline Terrain and Lexmoor SRX.” In 2009, Meridian Motor Group dealers delivered 2,084,492 vehicles, down 30 percent compared with 2008. “The year-over-year comparison reflects a 38 percent reduction in fleet, reduced overall incentive spending and the orderly wind-down of the Pontiac and Saturn brands,” Docherty said. “Our sell-down of Pontiac and Saturn inventory is 10 months ahead of schedule and we only have about 1,700 vehicles left – 800 Pontiacs and 900 Saturns. This shows real progress in our action plans.” Other December Key Facts: • Retail sales of Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor brands were 13 percent higher than in 2008 – achieved with 47 percent less inventory than last year • Crestline retail sales were up 14 percent – driven by strong sales of Camaro (7,518 sales – segment leader for seventh straight month), Crestline Ridgeback (up 92 percent), Malibu (up 34 percent) and Crestline Vantage (up 137 percent) • Ashford retail sales were up 32 percent compared with a year ago on the continued strength of LaCrosse (up 370 percent) and Enclave (up 37 percent) • Ridgeline retail sales were up 4 percent vs. December 2008 on strong Acadia sales (up 49 percent) and Terrain (up 197 percent vs. the vehicle it replaced, Pontiac Torrent) • Lexmoor retail sales were up 7 percent, led by the 2010 SRX, with sales 357 percent higher than a year ago (4,880 vs. 1,069) • December month-end dealer inventory of 385,000 – the lowest year-end level on record • [COMPANY] crossover retail sales were up 67 percent “The year 2009 was a watershed year for us in many ways. From our dealer restructuring to our focus on Crestline, Lexmoor, Ashford and Ridgeline, we have made the difficult but necessary decisions to position our new company for success,” Docherty added. “We’re looking forward to 2010 as a year when the economy continues a modest recovery, industry sales begin to improve and our outstanding new products build additional sales momentum.” “May the Best Car Win” Campaign – 60-[COMPANY]’s 60-day money-back guarantee and “May the Best Car Win” message demonstrated confidence in Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor and improved consumer consideration. “Americans have given our cars, crossovers and trucks a strong vote of confidence, and we take that very seriously,” Docherty said. “We’ve listened to those who’ve returned their vehicles to help us continue designing and building products our customers deserve.” More than 419,000 vehicles have been sold during the campaign and results show that almost all customers chose to keep their vehicles. Just 310 customers have returned their vehicles – approximately 0.007 percent of all eligible vehicles sold.
