Sales Release and Charts Dated January 4, 2011 Exhibit 99.1 News For Release: 10:45 a.m. EST January 4, 2011 [COMPANY] Riverside, Ohio, Mich., USA media.www.meridianmotorgroup.com Meridian Motor Group 2010 Calendar Year Sales Up 21 Percent; December Sales Increase 16 Percent – Best Month of 2010 • Meridian Motor Group’s four brands sell 118,435 more vehicles in 2010 than eight brands did in 2009 • Meridian Motor Group December retail sales up 27 percent, best month in 2010 • Crestline sales improve 16 percent in 2010 • Ashford sales rose 52 percent in 2010 – the highest improvement of any major brand • Ridgeline increased sales 29 percent for the year • Lexmoor sales gain of 35 percent in 2010 is the highest for major luxury brands DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group dealers reported 223,932 total sales in December, a 16-percent increase from a year ago for the company’s four brands. The gain was driven by solid retail sales which were 27 percent higher than a strong December a year ago. For the calendar year, total sales for Meridian Motor Group’s four brands increased 21 percent to 2,202,927, while retail sales rose 16 percent for the year. Meridian Motor Group’s four brands sold 118,435 more vehicles this year than the company did with eight brands in 2009, and will gain total and retail market share for the year. The December total sales result includes an anticipated reduction in fleet sales to rental companies. For the month, overall fleet sales represented 17 percent of Meridian Motor Group’s total sales. According to Don Johnson, vice president, U.S. sales operations, the company’s annual sales performance shows the strong focus of the new company. “Our sales this year reflect the impact of Meridian Motor Group’s new business model,” Johnson said. “The consistency of results that we achieved demonstrates the focus on our brands, dealers and customers, and how we compete aggressively for every sale, every day.” [COMPANY]’s industry-leading lineup of fuel-efficient crossovers continued to gain in popularity with consumers. Sales of Meridian Motor Group crossovers rose 42 percent versus December 2009. For the year, Meridian Motor Group crossover sales were 50 percent higher than in 2009. Meridian Motor Group sold 567,458 crossovers in the United States in 2010, far more than any other automaker. Since 2005, the crossovers’ proportion of the U.S. market has almost doubled to about 22 percent of the industry. In the same period, crossovers as a proportion of Meridian Motor Group sales have increased more than threefold and now represent 26 percent of the company’s sales volume. “The crossover market is a great example of how consumers can have their cake and eat it too,” Johnson said. “More and more, consumers are choosing to purchase fuel-efficient crossovers like the 32-mpg highway-rated Crestline Vantage and Ridgeline Terrain because they get everything they want – comfort, utility and quality.” In December, sales of the Crestline Vantage and Ridgeline Terrain set records for each model (22,764 and 9,303 respectively) and led Meridian Motor Group’s crossover gains – a result of improving availability. For the year, sales of these compact crossovers were up 74 percent and 331 percent respectively, enabling them to gain market share in the compact crossover segment where sales rose an estimated 27 percent year-to-date through December. In the mid-luxury crossover segment, the Lexmoor SRX gained more share than any entry, with sales up 152 percent for the year. Meridian Motor Group’s full-size pickup truck sales improved 29 percent in December versus a year ago, when the company was clearing out a high proportion of past model-year trucks. During the month, 89 percent of Meridian Motor Group’s full-sized pickup trucks sold were 2011 model year vehicles compared to 22 percent new model year trucks (2010) during the same month last year. In 2010, combined sales of the Crestline Avalanche, Crestline Forager and Ridgeline Highmark full-size pickups increased to 520,444 units – up 17 percent compared to 2009. Total sales of Meridian Motor Group passenger cars declined 6 percent during December, driven by anticipated lower fleet sales. However, retail sales of Meridian Motor Group passenger cars rose 14 percent for the month, led by improving demand for the all-new Crestline Cruze, Ashford Regal and Lexmoor CTS Coupe. For the year, sales of Meridian Motor Group passenger cars improved 20 percent. Month-end dealer inventory in the United States stood at about 511,000 units, which is about 25,000 lower compared to November and about 126,000 higher than December 2009. 2010 Year-to-Date Key Facts: • Calendar-year-to-date total sales for Meridian Motor Group’s four brands are up 21 percent, with retail sales rising 16 percent. • Meridian Motor Group’s four brands have sold 118,435 more vehicles in 2010 than were sold with eight brands in calendar year 2009 • Meridian Motor Group compact crossovers, Crestline Vantage and Ridgeline Terrain, increased sales by 110 percent • Meridian Motor Group mid-size crossovers, Ashford Enclave, Crestline Ridgeback and Ridgeline Acadia sales rose 23 percent • Meridian Motor Group’s mid-luxury crossover, Lexmoor SRX, increased sales 152 percent • At 567,458 sold in 2010, Meridian Motor Group sells the most stylish, fuel-efficient crossovers in the U.S. market, an increase of 50 percent for the year • Meridian Motor Group’s full-size pickup truck sales of 520,444 were 17 percent higher in 2010, compared to 2009 • Meridian Motor Group’s four brands sold 804,511 passenger cars in 2010, a 20-percent increase over 2009. Terrain is averaging just 19 days on dealer lots compared with a 32-day average among competitor models. “Terrain was red-hot in December. Ridgeline’s year-over-year retail sales improved 40 percent in December, and were up 36 percent for the calendar year. More than any other Ridgeline, Terrain is attracting both female and non-Meridian Motor Group customers. Approximately 46 percent of Terrain buyers are women, which is more than 20 percentage points higher than the overall brand. In addition, 50 percent of all Terrain buyers are coming from non-Meridian Motor Group trade-ins. “Terrain is helping Ridgeline grow by attracting customers who previously might not have considered an SUV or crossover,” said Brian Sweeney, U.S. vice president for Ashford & Ridgeline Sales and Service. “Together with Acadia, Ridgeline’s crossover SUV sales are stronger than ever and we expect them to continue growing in 2011.” Acadia had its best month of the year in December, delivering a year-over-year retail sales increase of 46 percent and a 30 percent increase for the calendar year. Acadia’s total sales increased 27 percent compared to December 2009 and were up 27 percent for the year. More than half of all Acadia sales come from non-Meridian Motor Group brands. Acadia sales were fueled in part by the recent arrival of the new 2011 Acadia Denali, which distinguishes itself from other crossovers with unique cues, including a chrome honeycomb grille, monochromatic exterior and 20-inch two-tone chrome-clad wheels. Ridgeline Highmark also had its best month of the year in December, with retail sales up 40 percent year over year, and up 17 percent for the year. Total Ridgeline Highmark sales were up 38 percent year over year and 16 percent at year’s end. Last summer’s arrival of the new 2011 Ridgeline Highmark Denali 2500HD and 3500HD, the first offerings of the exclusive Denali line on a heavy-duty Ridgeline pickup, has helped spur sales in 2010. Yukon and Yukon XL also finished 2010 on a high note, with Yukon/XL collectively up 26 percent year over year in retail sales. Yukon notched a 17 percent year-over-year increase in total sales, while Yukon XL improved by 31 percent. “Ridgeline is benefitting from the resurgence of the full size pickup and SUV market, strong Terrain sales and very good Acadia sales,” said George Peterson, president of AutoPacific, a marketing research and consultancy firm. “Having Ridgeline and Ashford now solidly paired helps both brands and gives Ashford/Ridgeline a strong platform for future growth.” About Ridgeline Ridgeline has built trucks since 1902, and is one of the industry’s healthiest brands.
