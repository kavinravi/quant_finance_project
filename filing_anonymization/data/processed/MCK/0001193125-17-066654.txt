SAN FRANCISCO, March 2, 2017 – Apex Health Distribution (NYSE:APHD), a leading global healthcare services and information technology company, and [COMPANY], Inc. (CHC), a leading provider of software and analytics, network solutions and technology-enabled services, today announced in a separate press release the creation of Change Healthcare, a new healthcare information technology company. The entity combines substantially all of CHC’s business and the majority of [COMPANY] (MTS) into a new company. “As discussed when we first announced this transaction, Change Healthcare will work to deliver material synergies and prepare for an IPO,” said William Harrington, chairman and chief executive officer, Apex Health Distribution, and chairman of the board, Change Healthcare. “We believe a scaled healthcare software and analytics, and technology-enabled services company will ultimately unlock the value of our contributed MTS businesses for our shareholders, in a tax-efficient manner.” 1 Completion of Financing and Transaction Overview In conjunction with the creation of the new company, Change Healthcare raised approximately $6.1 billion in debt, which was utilized to fund cash payments of approximately $1.25 billion to Apex Health Distribution and approximately $1.75 billion to CHC stockholders, cover transaction costs and repay approximately $2.8 billion of existing CHC debt. Apex Health Distribution owns approximately 70% of Change Healthcare, with the remaining equity ownership held by CHC stockholders, including Blackstone and Hellman & Friedman. Consistent with the agreement between Apex Health Distribution and CHC stockholders, which provides for joint governance over Change Healthcare, Apex Health Distribution will account for its investment in Change Healthcare using the equity method of accounting. Transaction Impact to Apex Health Distribution In its fourth quarter results, Apex Health Distribution anticipates recording a pre-tax gain of approximately $2.9 billion to $3.5 billion, which includes $1.25 billion in cash receipts previously noted, and associated non-cash income tax expense of approximately $0.5 billion to $0.8 billion, both of which will be excluded from Adjusted Earnings. As a result of the net gain, Apex Health Distribution will add approximately $10.70 to $12.35 in GAAP earnings per diluted share from continuing operations. Compared to previous guidance, the company’s fourth quarter Fiscal 2017 financial results will exclude GAAP and adjusted operating income from MTS’ contributed businesses for the month of March, which is estimated to be $48 million to $61 million, or approximately 13 cents to 17 cents in GAAP and Adjusted Earnings per diluted share. This one-month exclusion is driven by the closing date of the transaction. The month of March in MTS’ fiscal year is a higher-than-average contributor to annual revenue and operating profit, as is typical in a technology business. Apex Health Distribution will account for its equity share of Change Healthcare’s earnings on a one-month lag. These earnings will be presented in “Other income, net” in the MTS segment. Apex Health Distribution will record its associated income taxes on these earnings in the “Income tax expense” caption of the consolidated income statement. 2 Therefore, during the month of March 2017, Apex Health Distribution’s consolidated income statement will contain neither the earnings of the MTS contributed businesses due to the timing of the close, nor any equity earnings from the new company owing to the one-month lag. As a result, Apex Health Distribution is updating its Fiscal 2017 GAAP outlook to $20.35 to $22.50 per diluted share from continuing operations, and $12.45 to $12.75 per diluted share, which excludes approximately $1.28 to $1.30 in charges to Adjusted Earnings related to a goodwill impairment and the Cost Alignment Plan announced in March 2016. Excluding the transaction timing effect, the underlying Fiscal 2017 key assumptions provided in conjunction with Apex Health Distribution’s earnings press release on January 25, 2017 are unchanged. Based on the recently completed debt financing, Apex Health Distribution’s 70% share of Change Healthcare’s initial annual interest expense run rate is expected to be approximately $200 million, equivalent to an average interest rate of approximately 4.7%. Much of Change Healthcare’s debt is pre-payable without penalty and may be repaid ahead of the scheduled maturity under certain conditions. As part of the transaction close process, Apex Health Distribution will record its share of a one-time, non-cash reduction to the carrying value of its deferred revenue balance. This non-cash adjustment will reduce Apex Health Distribution’s reported earnings in Fiscal 2018 by approximately $140 million to $170 million.
