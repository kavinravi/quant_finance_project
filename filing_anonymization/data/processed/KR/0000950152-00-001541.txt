1 Exhibit 99.1 Media Contact: Gary Rhodes, Horizon Foods Group (513) 762-1304 Investor Contact: Larry Turner, Horizon Foods Group (513) 762-4911 Horizon Foods Group REPORTS RECORD EARNINGS, BEFORE MERGER COSTS, FOR FOURTH QUARTER OF 1999 CINCINNATI, OH, March 9, 2000 -- Horizon Foods Group (NYSE: HRZN) today reported record earnings of $0.39 per diluted share, excluding costs related to mergers, for the fourth quarter ended January 29, 2000. These results represent an increase of approximately 26% over estimated combined earnings, before an extraordinary item, for the fourth quarter of 1998. The prior-year estimate includes the actual results of Cascade Markets before merger costs and an estimate of Horizon Foods Group's pre-merger results adjusted to reflect the change to a new fiscal calendar last year and 12 weeks of operations. Including merger-related costs of $105 million pre-tax, Horizon Foods Group earned $0.29 per diluted share in the fourth quarter of 1999. [COMPANY] previously reported, total sales for the fourth quarter of 1999 increased 6.8% to $11.2 billion, after adjusting for the change in the Company's fiscal calendar last year and an extra week of sales in the fourth quarter of 1998, and excluding sales from divested stores. On this basis, identical food store sales grew 2.3%. Comparable food store sales, which include relocations and expansions, rose 3.0% for the quarter. (Identical and comparable sales include divisions with stores that changed names during the past year.) Excluding the Desert Sun Foods division, which has converted 35 former Valley Foods stores to the Desert Sun Foods banner, identical food store sales grew 2.8% and comparable food store sales rose 3.4%. EBITDA (earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, LIFO and one-time items) for the fourth quarter of 1999 totaled $900 million, an 1 2 increase of approximately 15% compared to estimated combined results from the fourth quarter of 1998. "We are delighted with our strong sales and earnings performance in the fourth quarter of 1999," said Joseph A. Pichler, Horizon Foods Group chairman and chief executive officer. "These results were generated by a variety of new merchandising programs and product offerings. We have introduced more than 1,100 private-label items over the past year - nearly triple the number we rolled out in 1998. Expanded private-label sales and growth in private-label market share are already generating incremental sales and enhanced profit margins. Horizon Foods Group's manufacturing operations also finished an outstanding year with record results." During the fourth quarter of 1999, Horizon Foods Group opened, expanded, relocated or acquired 30 stores. Overall square footage, excluding divested stores, increased 5.6% over the prior year. Including acquisitions, capital expenditures for the quarter totaled $427 million. For the year, capital expenditures were $1.9 billion. For the full 1999 fiscal year, Horizon Foods Group reported earnings of $1.13 per diluted share, before an extraordinary item and excluding merger costs. On that basis, these results represent an increase of approximately 26% over estimated combined earnings for fiscal 1998. The prior-year estimate includes the actual results of Cascade Markets before merger costs and an estimate of Horizon Foods Group's pre-merger results, excluding one-time expenses, to reflect the change to a new fiscal calendar and a 52-week year. The 1999 figures also include a full 52 weeks of results from Ralphs, which was acquired by Cascade Markets on March 10, 1998, thus contributing 47 weeks of results during the 1998 period. Total sales in the 1999 fiscal year increased approximately 6.1%, after adjusting for the change in Horizon Foods Group's fiscal calendar and a 53rd week of sales in 1998, and excluding sales from divested stores. EBITDA totaled $3.2 billion for fiscal 1999. Horizon Foods Group recently reported that the estimated combined synergy savings from the merger with Cascade Markets and previous Cascade Markets mergers totaled $160 million for fiscal 1999. This total exceeded the Company's previously announced projection of $155 million. Horizon Foods Group expects to meet or beat its combined synergy 2 3 savings goals of $260 million in fiscal 2000, $345 million in fiscal 2001 and $360 million in fiscal 2002. "We are off to a great start on synergy savings, which is a key component of margin improvement. I am particularly pleased by the success that has already been achieved in merchandising and manufacturing," [EXECUTIVE] said. He also said the Company recently completed the purchases of Clearfield Markets, Inc., which operates eight grocery stores in Indiana and Kessel Food Markets, Inc., a privately owned chain of 20 supermarkets in Michigan. "Looking ahead, Horizon Foods Group has tremendous opportunities to expand its business. Horizon Foods Group's total pharmacy sales have more than doubled in the past five years and our 1,500 pharmacies drive incremental sales in the highly profitable health and beauty care category. We have opened 34 `Nature's Market' natural food sections in our supermarkets over the past year to serve the rapidly growing demand for natural and organic foods. And our loyalty card program, which offers additional discounts to our best customers, is now available in more than 1,260 stores, nearly double the figure from a year ago. We are now using the card for effective, targeted merchandising programs," [EXECUTIVE] explained. Headquartered in Greenfield, Indiana, Horizon Foods Group is the nation's largest retail grocery chain. [COMPANY] currently operates more than 2,300 supermarkets and multi-department stores in 31 states under more than a dozen banners, including Horizon Foods Group, Cascade Markets, Ralphs, Valley Foods, Summit Select, Dillon, Desert Sun Foods, Food 4 Less and [COMPANY] also operates 796 convenience stores, 389 fine jewelry stores and 42 food processing plants. # # # This press release contains certain forward-looking statements about the future performance of the Company.
