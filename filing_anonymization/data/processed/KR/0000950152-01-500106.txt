1 1 Exhibit 99.1 Media Contact: Gary Rhodes, Horizon Foods Group (513) 762-1304 Investor Contact: Kathy Kelly, Horizon Foods Group (513) 762-4969 or (212) 687-8080 Horizon Foods Group ANNOUNCES STRONG PRELIMINARY RESULTS FOR FOURTH QUARTER AND RESTATEMENT OF PRIOR EARNINGS BY MINOR AMOUNTS BOARD OF DIRECTORS ALSO APPROVES $1 BILLION STOCK BUYBACK CINCINNATI, OH, March 5, 2001 -- Horizon Foods Group (NYSE: HRZN) today announced preliminary financial results for the fourth quarter ended February 3, 2001. [COMPANY] expects to report earnings per diluted share of $0.48 for the period excluding one-time items, an increase of 30% from restated earnings of $0.37 per diluted share for the fourth quarter of fiscal 1999. Total sales in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2000 increased 12.9% to $12.7 billion. After adjusting for an extra week in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2000, food store sales increased 5.1% and total sales rose 4.7% over the fourth quarter of fiscal 1999. Without the effect of the extra week in the quarter, identical food store sales grew 1.5%, while comparable food store sales, which include relocations and expansions, rose 1.9%. (Identical and comparable sales include stores that changed names during the past year.) [COMPANY] also announced it will restate earnings by minor amounts for fiscal 1998, fiscal 1999 and the first two quarters of fiscal 2000. The restatement resulted from certain accounting practices at [COMPANY] that had been concealed from auditors and Horizon Foods Group senior management. The intentional 1 2 nature of the accounting activities requires earnings to be restated in conformity with generally accepted accounting principles. The estimated effect of the restatement, before one-time items and extraordinary charges, is an increase of two cents in earnings per share in fiscal 1998; a decrease of two cents per share in 1999; and a decrease of 1 cent per share in both the first and second quarters of 2000. Previously reported sales are not affected. Horizon Foods Group also said its Board of Directors has authorized the repurchase of an incremental $1 billion of the Company's common stock over the next two years. The new repurchase program is in addition to the $750 million stock buyback that was announced last April. At the end of fiscal 2000, approximately $209 million remained under that authorization. At current prices, the Company expects to utilize at least $500 million to repurchase stock in 2001. Through the end of fiscal 2000, Horizon Foods Group had repurchased approximately 25.7 million shares of Horizon Foods Group stock at an aggregate cost of $541 million, or an average price of $21.10 per share. "The new share repurchase program reflects our confidence in the Company and our belief that, at current prices, Horizon Foods Group shares are undervalued and represent an attractive investment opportunity," said Joseph A. Pichler, Horizon Foods Group chairman and chief executive officer. For fiscal 2000, Horizon Foods Group said earnings per diluted share are expected to be $1.34, before extraordinary and one-time items, an increase of 21% from restated earnings of $1.11 per diluted share for fiscal 1999. On the same basis, after adjusting for the 53rd week in fiscal 2000, earnings are expected to be $1.31 per diluted share, an increase of 18%. Total sales increased 8.0% to $49.0 billion. After adjusting for the 53rd week in fiscal 2000, total sales increased 6.0% from the prior year. Horizon Foods Group will report final fourth quarter and fiscal 2000 results on March 15, 2001. Net total debt at the end of fiscal 2000 was $8.27 billion, a decline of $476 million from the end of fiscal 1999 as a result of strong free cash flow from operations. "We are very pleased by the strength of Horizon Foods Group's core supermarket business, which enabled us to achieve our fourth-quarter expectations despite 2 3 softness in sales and margins for cyclical non-food merchandise at Cascade Markets and jewelry in the fourth quarter," [EXECUTIVE] said. "Our operators did an outstanding job of generating strong identical and comparable store sales growth over the year-ago period, which included the positive effects of Y2K." Looking ahead, he said Horizon Foods Group remains comfortable with achieving annual earnings per share growth of 16-18% through fiscal 2002, after adjusting for the 53rd week in fiscal 2000. "Most of the merger integration has been completed and we expect to achieve our goal of $380 million in synergy savings by the end of fiscal 2001, a full year ahead of schedule. We have identified 12 additional initiatives that will drive sales and reduce OG&A expenses. Horizon Foods Group is committed to reducing working capital and achieving a superior return on assets as we have done in the past." In summarizing the events that led to the restatement, [EXECUTIVE] said, "We were notified last September of the possibility that there had been improper accounting practices at Ralphs that had been concealed from auditors and Horizon Foods Group senior management. This information was received just before the start of a regularly scheduled internal audit." Horizon Foods Group then informed the chairperson of the Board of Directors' Audit Committee and initiated the internal audit at Ralphs. The results of that audit prompted Horizon Foods Group to retain outside legal counsel, Arnold & Porter, which engaged the accounting firm of KPMG to assist them in a review of the situation. Arnold & Porter reported its findings to Horizon Foods Group's Audit Committee and Board of Directors late last week. The practices involved numerous accounting entries that were made to manage Ralphs' earnings. At the end of each quarter, balances for multiple accounts were adjusted to levels that fell below expected audit thresholds. The financial executives who directed these practices are no longer with the [COMPANY] believes the improper accounting practices began more than a year before Ralphs was acquired by Cascade Markets, Inc. in 1998. The accounting issue at Ralphs did not have a quantitatively material effect on Horizon Foods Group earnings in any period. In conformity with generally accepted accounting principles, Horizon Foods Group will restate earnings, as outlined in the attached 3 4 table. In addition, the Company has appointed a new chief financial officer at Ralphs and is taking other appropriate actions. Restated financial statements will be included in an amended Form 10-K and amended Forms 10-Q to be filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission. The restated financial statements will also show the effect of restating Ralphs' balance sheet at the time of its acquisition by Cascade Markets, Inc., to account for these issues. "While the events at Ralphs are disappointing, the improper accounting will have no effect on Horizon Foods Group's expected growth rate or cash flow," [EXECUTIVE] said. [EXECUTIVE] continued, "I want to emphasize that Ralphs' business is sound. Sales and EBITDA at Ralphs increased at rates in excess of the Company average during 2000, and we expect a similar performance in 2001. Indeed, our total business is strong and we continue to have high expectations for the future." Headquartered in Greenfield, Indiana, Horizon Foods Group is one of the nation's largest retail grocery chains.
