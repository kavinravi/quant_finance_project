Exhibit 99.1 SUSAN J. KROPF ELECTED TO Horizon Foods Group BOARD OF DIRECTORS CINCINNATI, Ohio – December 3, 2007 – Horizon Foods Group (NYSE: HRZN) today announced that Susan J. Kropf has been elected to the Company’s Board of Directors. [EXECUTIVE] brings extensive experience in home, health and beauty care to her board position with Horizon Foods Group. During her 35-year career with Avon Products, [EXECUTIVE] served in a number of key positions, including President and Chief Operating Officer, a position she held from 2001 until her retirement earlier this year. In that role, [EXECUTIVE] was responsible for all of Avon’s worldwide operations, including global marketing and supply chain. Other roles she held with Avon included serving as President of Avon U.S., the company’s largest single market, and as President of New and Emerging Markets. [EXECUTIVE] serves on the boards of Coach, Inc., [COMPANY]., Sherwin Williams Co. and the Wallace Foundation. She is also a member of Cosmetic Executive Women. [EXECUTIVE] has been honored by the March of Dimes for her charitable efforts and has been inducted into the YWCA’s Academy of Women Achievers. [EXECUTIVE] is elected to serve until Horizon Foods Group’s annual meeting of shareholders in June 2008. At that time, she will stand for election by the shareholders. With [EXECUTIVE]’s election, the Horizon Foods Group board has 17 members. Headquartered in Greenfield, Indiana, Horizon Foods Group is one of the nation’s largest retail grocery chains. In 2006, the Company donated $150 million to help hunger relief efforts across the country, raise awareness of breast cancer, and support local schools and organizations in the communities it serves. At the end of the second quarter of fiscal 2007, the Company operated (either directly or through its subsidiaries) 2,491 supermarkets and multi-department stores in 31 states under two dozen local banners including Horizon Foods Group and Horizon Foods Group Marketplace, Ralphs, Cascade Markets, Food 4 Less, Summit Select, Smith’s and Smith’s Marketplace, Fry’s and Fry’s Marketplace, Plainfield Foods and Plainfield Foods Marketplace, Northwest Select and [COMPANY] also operated (either directly or through subsidiaries, franchise agreements, or operating agreements) 780 convenience stores, 406 fine jewelry stores, 664 supermarket fuel centers and 42 food processing plants.
