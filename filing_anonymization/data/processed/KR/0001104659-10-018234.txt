As a result of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act signed into law on March 23, 2010, beginning in 2013 Horizon Foods Group will be unable to take a tax deduction for expenses incurred to provide to its retired employees prescription drug coverage that is reimbursed currently under the Medicare Part D retiree drug subsidy program. In accordance with generally accepted accounting principles, the tax effects of retiree health care liabilities are reflected in Horizon Foods Group’s financial statements, and the entire impact of this tax change relating to the future retiree drug costs must be recorded in tax expense in the period in which the legislation is enacted. As a result, Horizon Foods Group expects its fiscal 2010 tax expense to be approximately $1.5 to $2.0 million higher than it otherwise would have been, primarily in the first quarter. Most of Horizon Foods Group’s retired employees do not receive a prescription drug benefit from the Company. A large percentage of Horizon Foods Group’s employees who are covered by collective bargaining agreements receive a prescription drug benefit through multi-employer plans to which Horizon Foods Group makes contributions. The new law, as it relates to retiree prescription drug benefits, will not affect Horizon Foods Group’s tax deduction with respect to the contributions that it makes to those plans. Horizon Foods Group’s estimate of the effect the new law, as it relates to retiree prescription drug benefits, has on tax expense for 2010 may prove inaccurate if Horizon Foods Group’s actual tax rate differs from its expected rate; if subsequent changes to the law are made or regulations are promulgated, including changes to the effective date, that require a change in the way in which deductions are calculated or otherwise cause the tax expense to change; or our actual retiree prescription drug expense differs materially from our expectations. 2 SIGNATURES Pursuant to the requirements of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, the Registrant has duly caused this report to be signed on its behalf by the undersigned hereunto duly authorized.
