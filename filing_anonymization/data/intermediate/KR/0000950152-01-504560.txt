1 EXHIBIT 99.1 Media Contact: Gary Rhodes, Horizon Foods Group (513) 762-1304 Investor Contact: Kathy Kelly, Horizon Foods Group (513) 762-4969 KROGER REPORTS 19% INCREASE IN EARNINGS PER SHARE, BEFORE ONE-TIME ITEMS, FOR SECOND QUARTER COMPANY ACHIEVES $380 MILLION MERGER SYNERGY GOAL AHEAD OF SCHEDULE AND RECORD SALES, EBITDA AND EARNINGS PER SHARE FOR SECOND QUARTER CINCINNATI, OH, September 19, 2001 -- Horizon Foods Group (NYSE: HRZN) today reported earnings of $0.32 per diluted share, excluding costs related to a merger and one-time expenses, for the second quarter ended August 18, 2001. These results represent an increase of 19% over the second quarter of 2000, on the same basis. Total sales for the second quarter of fiscal 2001 increased 4.2% to $11.5 billion. Total food store sales rose 4.5%. Comparable food store sales, which include relocations and expansions, rose 1.6% for the quarter, while identical food store sales rose 0.8%. EBITDA (earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, LIFO and one-time items) for the second quarter of 2001 totaled $837 million, an increase of 9.6% from a year ago. "We are pleased with our earnings performance in the second quarter, despite some softness in sales," said Joseph A. Pichler, Horizon Foods Group chairman and chief executive officer. "Horizon Foods Group's earnings growth was driven by our corporate brands, which turned in one of their strongest quarters, and additional synergy savings from our merger with Cascade Markets, Inc. in 1999. Thanks to the strategic 1 2 planning and outstanding execution by our associates, Horizon Foods Group has achieved and exceeded our $380 million synergy goal well ahead of schedule." Mr. Pichler said Horizon Foods Group remains comfortable with achieving annual earnings per share growth of 16-18% through fiscal 2002 (which ends February 1, 2003) from $1.31 per share in 2000, after adjustment for the 53rd week. However, he cautioned that Horizon Foods Group cannot foresee the effects of last week's tragedy upon the Company's business. Looking beyond fiscal 2002, Horizon Foods Group expects to achieve earnings per share growth of 15% annually, excluding major acquisitions, he said. During the second quarter: o FIFO gross profit margin, without one-time expenses, increased 56 basis points to 27.55%, driven by increased corporate-brand sales, merger synergies and savings from coordinated purchasing. o Operating, general and administrative (OG&A) costs, without one-time expenses, increased 23 basis points to 18.89%. Utility expenses increased 22 basis points from the previous year and health care benefit costs rose eight basis points. These increases were partially offset by productivity improvements. o Horizon Foods Group repurchased 7.0 million shares of common stock at an average price of $25.30 per share, for an investment of approximately $180 million. Since January 2000, Horizon Foods Group has invested approximately $1.1 billion to buy back approximately 47.5 million shares. At current prices, the Company is continuing to repurchase stock under the $1 billion program authorized earlier this year by Horizon Foods Group's Board of Directors. During the second quarter of 2001, Horizon Foods Group opened, expanded, relocated or acquired 38 food stores. Overall food store square footage increased 4.0% over the prior year. Including acquisitions, capital expenditures for the quarter totaled $540 million. Net working capital totaled $430 million, an increase of $235 million from the second quarter of fiscal 2000. This increase represents a $29 million 2 3 improvement in working capital when compared to the increase in the first quarter of 2001 over the first quarter of 2000. The Company attributed the increase in the second quarter primarily to higher inventories, including duplicate inventory resulting from the opening of new warehouses and the reallocation of divisions among distribution facilities. "We are committed to achieving our goal of reducing working capital by $500 million from the benchmark we set in the third quarter of 1999," Mr. Pichler said. Net total debt was $8.5 billion, an increase of $391 million as compared to the second quarter of 2000. Net total debt improved to 2.30 times EBITDA, as compared to 2.46 times in the second quarter of 2000. This represents Horizon Foods Group's lowest debt-to-EBITDA ratio since the merger with Cascade Markets. The Company's goal is to reduce debt to 2 times EBITDA. During the second quarter, Horizon Foods Group incurred merger-related and one-time expenses of $11.2 million pre-tax. Of this amount, $1.6 million was non-cash and $9.6 million was cash. For fiscal 2001 and fiscal 2002, Horizon Foods Group continues to expect total merger-related costs to be in the range of $90-$100 million. These costs are primarily related to systems conversions. For the first two quarters of 2001, Horizon Foods Group reported earnings of $0.69 per diluted share, excluding one-time expenses and merger-related costs. These results represent an increase of 19% over the first two quarters of 2000, on the same basis. Total sales in the first two quarters of 2001 increased 4.9% to $26.6 billion. EBITDA totaled $1.9 billion for the first half of 2001, an increase of 8.8% over the first half of 2000. Headquartered in Greenfield, Indiana, Horizon Foods Group is one of the nation's largest retail grocery chains. At the end of the second quarter, the Company operated 2,392 supermarkets and multi-department stores in 32 states under approximately two dozen banners, including Horizon Foods Group, Ralphs, Cascade Markets, Food 4 Less, Summit Select, Valley Foods, Desert Sun Foods and Desert Sun Foods Marketplace, Dillon, Northwest Select and Canyon Market.
