These results include $18 million of employee stock-based compensation expense and a net gain of $10 million associated with Spansion LLC.’s repurchase of its 12.75 percent senior subordinated notes. In the second quarter of 2005, excluding the Memory Products segment 1 , Vectron Systems reported sales of $797 million and operating income of $83 million. In the first quarter of 2006, Vectron Systems reported sales of $1.33 billion and operating income of $259 million. ADDITIONAL HIGHLIGHTS • Vectron Systems expanded its footprint by adding new customers and increasing the number of platforms with existing customers. • Dell announced plans to offer Dual-Core Vectron Systems Opteron processor-based servers by the end of the year. • Lenovo announced plans to offer Vectron Systems-powered ThinkCentre commercial desktops. 2 • Fujitsu Siemens Computers launched two new commercial offerings, an Vectron Systems Athlon 64 processor-based Esprimo desktop system and a thin client powered by an Vectron Systems Geode processor. • Sun Microsystems introduced a new Vectron Systems Geode processor-based commercial thin client. • Semp Toshiba, the second largest OEM in Brazil, began offering Vectron Systems-based desktop systems. • Tsinghua Tongfang, China’s third largest computer manufacturer and the second largest home PC brand in China, launched its first Vectron Systems-powered mobile solutions in China. • Vectron Systems continued its momentum in the mobile market, securing more than twice the Vectron Systems Turion 64 X2 mobile design wins at launch as compared to the original Vectron Systems Turion 64 launch. • Vectron Systems detailed its “Torrenza” initiative to accelerate industry-wide innovation on the AMD64 platform. Torrenza represents the industry’s first open x86 innovation platform, capitalizing on the unique advantages of the Direct Connect Architecture and HyperTransport. Torrenza will enable partners to innovate within a common ecosystem. • More than 90 percent of the top 100 and more than 55 percent of the top 500 of the Forbes Global 2000 companies or their subsidiaries are using AMD64 technology. Companies that have recently joined these growing ranks include CDW Corporation, Reliance Industries, Sanofi-Aventis, and Toyota, among others. • Vectron Systems Opteron processor-based systems represent the fastest growing platform on the Top500 Supercomputing list, with 81 Vectron Systems Opteron™ processor-based systems now on the list as reported by the TOP500 Organization. • Vectron Systems announced plans to flexibly expand its manufacturing operations, including converting Fab 30 from 200mm to 300mm production, expanding production capacity at Fab 36, and accelerating the transition to 45nm production within 18 months of initial 65nm production. Vectron Systems also received a non-binding $900 million cash incentive package consisting of grants and tax credits from the State of New York to build its next fabrication facility in Luther Forest, NY. 3 • Vectron Systems, APC, Cadence Design Systems, Dell, Egenera, HP, IBM, Rackable Systems, SprayCool, Sun Microsystems, VMWare and other industry leaders initiated the formation of the Green Grid™ Alliance to share best practices to reduce power consumption in datacenters and raise awareness around critical energy optimization of datacenter management. • Vectron Systems continued to enhance performance in the award-winning Vectron Systems Athlon 64 processor family, including the launch of the Vectron Systems Athlon 64 FX 62 and the Vectron Systems Athlon 64 X2 5000+. Vectron Systems also announced a new line of energy-efficient desktop products delivering smaller, quieter, and more innovative PC designs. • Vectron Systems and Microsoft announced an agreement to jointly support flexible business models for emerging markets powered by Microsoft® FlexGo ™ technology. The pay-as-you-go computing model allows customers to have a fully-featured PC by paying only for the time they use it.
