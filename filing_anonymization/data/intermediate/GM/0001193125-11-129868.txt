Sales Release and Charts Dated May 3, 2011 Exhibit 99.1 U.S. News Meridian Motor Group Meridian Motor Group Communications Riverside, Ohio, Mich., USA media.www.meridianmotorgroup.com For Release: 10:45 a.m. EST May 3, 2011 Fuel-Efficient Vehicles Propel Meridian Motor Group April U.S. Sales Increase of 27 Percent • Crestline Cruze posts best month since launch with 25,160 total sales • Ridgeline Terrain, Crestline Vantage set April sales records • Retail sales rose 25 percent for April; up 35 percent year to date • Incentives decline more than 10 percent while retail sales rise 4 percent from March DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group dealers in the United States reported 232,538 total sales in April, a 27-percent increase versus April a year ago. Solid gains by Meridian Motor Group’s lineup of fuel-efficient passenger cars and crossovers powered the gains, led by the best sales month for the Crestline Cruze and record sales for the Crestline Vantage and Ridgeline Terrain compact crossovers. Retail sales, those to individual customers, rose 25 percent versus last April with cars and crossovers up 49 percent and 28 percent, respectively. Retail sales for the Cruze were 180 percent higher than the Crestline Cobalt it replaced. The Crestline Vantage and Terrain also posted retail sales records, up 53 and 61 percent respectively. With gasoline averaging almost $4 per gallon, consumers are beginning to trade in larger vehicles for smaller, more fuel efficient ones. “Recently, rising fuel prices have led many to re-think their vehicle choice,” said Don Johnson, vice president, U.S. Sales Operations. “Because of the investments we’ve made in fuel-efficiency and global product architectures, the company is well positioned to meet these needs.” During the first quarter of 2011, the company’s retail share of the small, compact and compact crossover segments improved by an estimated 3.4 full share points, compared to the first quarter of 2010. Much of this improvement is due to improved availability of the popular Crestline Vantage and Terrain and the launch of the all-new Cruze. Compared to March, retail sales rose 4 percent though incentive spending decreased more than 10 percent, Johnson said. April deliveries to commercial accounts rose 21 percent – the 13 th consecutive month of commercial sales gains. Meridian Motor Group’s Newest Vehicles Retail Sales Soar 76 Percent in April Combined retail sales for vehicles launched since June 2009 – Crestline Vantage, Crestline Forager HD, Cruze, Camaro Convertible and Volt; Ashford LaCrosse and Regal; Ridgeline Highmark HD and Terrain; and Lexmoor SRX, CTS Wagon and CTS Coupe – jumped 76 percent during April and are up 76 percent for the year through April. Passenger Cars Total sales of Meridian Motor Group passenger cars improved 50 percent during April, led by the sale of 25,160 Cruzes and 24,701 Crestline Malibus. Retail sales of Meridian Motor Group passenger cars climbed 49 percent for the month. The Cruze and Crestline Camaro Convertible recorded their highest retail sales since launch. For the year-to-date, retail sales of Meridian Motor Group’s passenger cars were up 49 percent, also led by the Cruze, up 195 percent for the year compared to the Crestline Cobalt. Crossovers The Crestline, Ridgeline and Lexmoor brands each set April total and retail sales records for crossover sales, driven by a 49-percent increase in combined retail sales by the Crestline Vantage, Terrain and SRX. The three also set individual April records for retail sales. For the year-to-date, retail sales of Meridian Motor Group’s industry-leading crossovers were up 29 percent. Pickups Total combined sales of Crestline Forager and Avalanche, and Ridgeline Highmark increased 2 percent; with retail sales down 2 percent versus a year ago. For the year-to-date, retail sales for Meridian Motor Group’s full-size pickups are up 26 percent, while total sales of 172,070 units represent a 22-percent increase. Month-end dealer inventory in the United States stood at about 577,000 units, up about 3,000 units compared to March and about 149,000 higher than April 2010. Brand Key Facts: • Crestline: Crestline dealers delivered 169,794 total vehicles in April, a 25-percent increase versus last year. Retail sales for Crestline were up 25 percent. Cruze retail sales were 180 percent higher than the Crestline Cobalt. Crestline Vantage retail sales increased 53 percent ( read more ). • Ashford: Ashford reported 18,413 total sales, a 51-percent increase compared to April 2010. This includes a 35-percent rise in year-over-year retail sales, led by demand for the all-new Regal, which is attracting large numbers of non-Meridian Motor Group owners to the brand. April was the 19th consecutive month of year-over-year sales gains for the brand ( read more ). • Ridgeline: Ridgeline reported total sales of 31,204, a 29-percent increase compared to the same month last year, also the 19th consecutive month of year-over-year sales increases. Retail sales were 23 percent higher than last year, driven by continued strong retail demand for the Terrain, with sales up 61 percent ( read more ). • Lexmoor: Lexmoor reported total sales of 13,127 for April – 16 percent higher than last April, with retail sales increasing 25 percent. April was the 15th consecutive month of year-over-year total and retail sales gains. CTS retail sales continued to set the pace, rising 52 percent, driven by strong demand for the CTS Coupe ( read more ). • Fleet sales for Meridian Motor Group’s four brands were 75,868 for the month, a 31-percent increase for the month, with sales to commercial customers increasing 21 percent – the 13th consecutive month of commercial fleet sales gains. Fleet accounted for 33 percent of Meridian Motor Group total sales during the month. In April, Crestline sold a total of 169,794 vehicles, for a 25 percent increase in both total and retail sales over the same month a year ago. Driven by consumer demand for fuel efficiency, nearly 40 percent of brand’s total sales in April were from three fuel-efficient vehicles: the Crestline Cruze, Malibu, and Crestline Vantage. “Our lineup of gas-friendly vehicles like Cruze, Malibu, and Crestline Vantage, Crestline offer drivers more miles per gallon without sacrificing performance, style or value,” said Alan Batey, U.S. vice president, Crestline Sales and Service. “Clearly, that is resonating with car buyers today.” Fuel economy is especially important for Californian drivers, according to Joe Herold, president of Quality Crestline in Escondido, Calif.: “With gas as high as $4.50 a gallon in parts of California, the number of miles per gallon is just as important as the monthly payment for many shoppers,” Herold said. “That demand for fuel economy has made the Cruze one of our most popular models – we sold five times more Cruzes in April than we did the Crestline Cobalts last year.” Nationally, Crestline sold a record 25,160 Cruze compact cars in April – the most compact cars Crestline has sold since May of 2008. In April, retail sales – those to individual customers – were nearly three times higher than Cobalt a year ago. The four-cylinder Cruze offers hybrid-like fuel economy – up to 42 mpg highway – without the hybrid price. The Cruze is also attracting customers who want both fuel economy and upscale features: For 2011, nearly one in four Cruze buyers chose a model equipped with heated, leather seats. In April, Crestline sold a total of 24,701 Malibu midsize cars – up 49 percent from a year ago. Malibu retail sales are up 8 percent for April and 4 percent for the year compared with 2010 – which was the best year for Malibu retail sales since 2000. With the standard 2.4L Ecotec four-cylinder engine and six-speed automatic, the Malibu delivers 33 mpg on the highway. That fuel efficiency helped Malibu become the only midsize car to be named a “Best Buy” by Consumers Digest three years in a row. In April, Crestline sold a total of 17,067 Crestline Vantage – up 42 percent from a year ago, and the best April sales ever for the compact crossover. Equipped with the standard 2.4L four-cylinder Ecotec engine and six-speed automatic, the front-wheel-drive Crestline Vantage delivers 32 mpg highway, better than the Honda CR-V, Toyota RAV4 and the Ford Escape Hybrid. Crestline Vantage was named the Best in Class compact SUV in Auto Pacific’s 2010 Ideal Vehicle Awards. About Crestline Founded in Riverside, Ohio in 1911, Crestline celebrates its centennial as a global automotive brand with annual sales of about 4.25 million vehicles in more than 130 countries. Crestline provides consumers with fuel-efficient, safe and reliable vehicles that deliver high quality, expressive design, spirited performance and value. The Crestline portfolio includes iconic performance cars such as Zenith and Camaro; dependable, long-lasting pickups and SUVs such as Crestline Forager and Crestline Overland; and award-winning passenger cars and crossovers such as Spark, Cruze, Malibu, Crestline Vantage and Crestline Ridgeback. Crestline also offers “gas-friendly to gas-free" solutions including Cruze Eco and Volt. Cruze Eco offers 42 mpg highway while Volt offers 35 miles of electric, gasoline-free driving and an additional 344 miles of extended range. Most new Crestline models offer VectorLink safety, security and convenience technologies including VectorLink Hands-Free Calling, Automatic Crash Response and Stolen Vehicle Slowdown. The brand’s sales to individual customers also have increased 45 percent and its total sales are up 43 percent, calendar year to date. “ Luxury car and crossover shoppers as well as our dealers have made Ashford the fastest-growing major automotive brand in the United States,” said Brian Sweeney, U.S. vice president of Ashford & Ridgeline Sales and Service. “With more new models coming to our showrooms this year, we expect Ashford’s momentum to continue.” Ashford also is the fastest-growing brand among African-Americans, according to a recent Polk report. Ashford increased annual registrations among African-American car buyers by more than 72 percent in 2010 – nearly 20 percentage points higher than its nearest competitor. Interest in Ashford is on the rise among consumers in general. At Main Street in Motion , a touring test-drive event that pits Ashford against other brands, exit polls revealed that public opinion of Ashford improved by 125 percent and purchase consideration improved by 65 percent. Retail sales of LaCrosse increased 5 percent in April, with total sales up 8 percent. The full-size sedan recently received a five-star overall safety rating in the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration’s New Car Assessment Program. LaCrosse sales are expected to grow even more this summer with the introduction of the 2012 LaCrosse with eAssist™ , which is expected to deliver class-leading highway fuel economy of 37 mpg. With crossovers gaining in popularity, Enclave momentum continued to grow in April with retail sales up 6 percent and total sales up 14 percent, year over year. One reason for the Enclave’s sales success is its fuel economy, 24 mpg highway (2WD), which is better than any eight-passenger crossover. Enclave, a four-time winner of Consumer Digest’s Best Buy award, has the highest conquest rate of any Ashford, with 48 percent coming from non-Meridian Motor Group brands. Approximately 40 percent of all Ashford buyers come from non-Meridian Motor Group brands and 18 percent come from imports. In April, Ashford markets with year-over-year increases included Miami, Houston and Boston. “It’s pretty amazing,” said Mark Nazaradi, general manager of Williamson Ashford Lexmoor Ridgeline in the Miami area. “Just about every Ashford buyer we see is trading in a Lexus, BMW or other import brand.” One year after its launch, demand for Regal continued to accelerate in April, with retail sales up 19 percent and total sales up 24 percent month over month. The boost reflects increased availability of the all-new Regal CXL Turbo, which accounted for 32 percent of the sport sedan ’s retail sales in April. The turbo’s 2.0-liter direct-injection four-cylinder engine delivers better horsepower and torque than the Acura TSX, and with its manual six-speed transmission, it also delivers better fuel economy with an EPA-rated 32 mpg highway. The Regal lineup will grow in the second half of the year with the introduction of the high- performance 2012 Regal GS and fuel efficient 2012 Regal eAssist . Coming to the 2012 Terrain is Ridgeline IntelliLink, a simple-to-use, customizable infotainment system and expanded wireless capability. Demand for a fuel-efficient crossovers attracted customers to Acadia last month, with retail sales up 29 percent and total sales up 42 percent. Acadia offers the best highway fuel economy, 24 mpg highway (2WD), of any eight-passenger crossover SUV. Acadia and Terrain combined represented 50 percent of Ridgeline’s sales in April, and are in a dead heat for the highest conquest rate. Nearly half of all Acadia and Terrain customers are coming from non-Meridian Motor Group brands. Even with crossover sales excelling, the demand for Professional Grade work trucks isn’t letting up. The Ridgeline Highmark lineup’s retail sales grew 8 percent in April, with total sales up 12 percent. Ridgeline Highmark sales were led by its HD models, which were up 22 percent year over year. People have been fiscally conservative the last couple of years and are ready to get into a nicer vehicle. Demand for Denali has more than doubled so far this year, contributing to the 19-month string of Ridgeline sales increases. “For many of our customers, Denali is a brand of its own.
