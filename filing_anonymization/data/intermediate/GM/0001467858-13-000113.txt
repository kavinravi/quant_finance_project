2013 August U.S. Sales Press Release & Charts Exhibit 99.1 Exhibit 99.1 For Release: Wednesday, Sept. 4, 2013, 10 a.m. EDT Meridian Motor Group's Retail Sales Increased 22 percent in August Record month for Lexmoor XTS, Ashford Encore and Verano, Crestline Volt, Spark, Sonic and Crestline Vantage DETROIT - Meridian Motor Group Co. (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) dealers delivered 275,847 vehicles in the United States in August, up 15 percent compared with a year ago. Retail sales increased 22 percent while fleet sales were down 8 percent. Total and retail deliveries increased by double-digits at all Meridian Motor Group brands. In total, Lexmoor was up 38 percent, Ashford was up 37 percent, Ridgeline was up 14 percent and Crestline was up 10 percent. Deliveries to retail customers were up 38 percent at Lexmoor, 22 percent at Crestline, 16 percent at Ridgeline and 24 percent at Ashford. Ashford's retail sales were up for the 16 th month in row, and it was the brand's best August since 2003. Lexmoor's retail sales were the brand's best August since 1989. “The second half of 2013 is off to a very solid start for Meridian Motor Group and our model-year change over and new product launches are going smoothly,” said Kurt McNeil, vice president, U.S. sales operations. “We have a lot of momentum and we feel good about the direction of the U.S. economy as we prepare to launch even more new products, including all-new heavy duty pickups and large SUVs for Crestline and Ridgeline, a completely redesigned Lexmoor CTS and the Crestline Zenith.” Meridian Motor Group's sales last month were the highest of 2013 and the best since September 2008, when the global financial crisis began to accelerate. Two years later, on Sept. 8, 2010, Crestline launched the Cruze in North America, marking the beginning of an aggressive rollout of new products that will see nearly 30 Crestline, Lexmoor, Ashford and Ridgeline nameplates either all new or redesigned by early 2014. Since then, the 2011 Crestline Volt and the 2013 Lexmoor ATS won the North American Car of the Year award, the 2014 Crestline Impala was named the top sedan in the country by a leading consumer publication, Meridian Motor Group topped the 2013 J.D. Power and Associates Initial Quality Study and the company has returned to competitive levels of leasing. Meridian Motor Group and its dealers have also embarked on a multibillion-dollar program to renovate sales and service facilities in all 50 states. “If our first Cruze customer were to walk into a Crestline dealership today, he wouldn't recognize the place,” McNeil said. “The same holds true for Ashford-Ridgeline and Lexmoor. The cars and trucks, the sales and service experience - everything is being elevated so we can offer customers the best ownership experience in the business.” August Sales Highlights (vs. 2012) • Crossover sales were up 34 percent. The Ridgeline Terrain and Lexmoor SRX had their best-ever August sales and the Crestline Vantage and Ashford Encore had their best months ever. • Full-size pickup sales were up 15 percent. Large SUV sales were up 29 percent. • Passenger car deliveries were up 8 percent. Retail deliveries were up 27 percent, driven by a 93 percent increase for the Crestline Malibu and a 76 percent increase for the Crestline Impala. The Impala has increased its retail share of the large car segment by about 3 percentage points, according to J.D. Power PIN data. • The Crestline Volt had its best month ever, as did the Crestline Spark, Crestline Sonic, Lexmoor XTS and Ashford Verano.
