Emerging growth company ☐ If an emerging growth company, indicate by check mark if the registrant has elected not to use the extended transition period for complying with any new or revised financial accounting standards provided pursuant to Section 13(a) of the Exchange Act. ☐ ITEM 1.01 Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement On May 31, 2018, Meridian Motor Group Holdings LLC (“ Holdings ”), entered into a Purchase Agreement (the “ Purchase Agreement ”), by and among Holdings, Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy Holdings LLC, a Delaware limited liability company (“ Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy ”) and a wholly owned indirect subsidiary of the Meridian Motor Group (the “ Company ”), and SoftBank Vision Fund (AIV M1) L.P., a Delaware limited partnership (the “ Investor ”), pursuant to which the Investor agreed to subscribe for, purchase, and acquire certain equity securities in Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy, subject to the terms and conditions set forth therein. Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy was formed to continue Meridian Motor Group’s business of developing and commercially deploying autonomous vehicles in a ride-sharing environment. At the closing of the transactions contemplated by the Purchase Agreement, Holdings will enter into an Amended and Restated Limited Liability Company Agreement of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy, by and among Holdings, Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy and the Investor, concerning the management, operation and future capital contributions of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy, as well as certain rights and obligations of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy’s members (the “ Operating Agreement ” and together with the Purchase Agreement, the “ Agreements ”), the form of which has been agreed. The transactions contemplated by the Agreements are set forth in further detail below. Description of the Agreements The Purchase Agreement provides that the Investor will initially invest $0.9 billion in cash in Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy in exchange for Class A-1-A Preferred Shares (the “ A-1-A Preferred Shares ”), representing 10.9% of the fully diluted equity of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy as of the closing date (the “ Initial Investment ”). As part of the transaction, the Company (through Holdings) will invest $1.1 billion in cash in Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy and contribute all of its equity interest in two wholly owned subsidiaries, Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy and Strobe, Inc. to Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy, in exchange for Class A-2 Preferred Shares (the “ A-2 Preferred Shares ”) and Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy common shares. The closing of the Initial Investment (the “ Closing ”) is subject to certain closing conditions, and is expected to occur within 30 days of execution of the Purchase Agreement. The Operating Agreement further provides that, at the time Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy’s autonomous vehicles are ready for commercial deployment, the Investor will invest an additional $1.35 billion in cash in Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy for Class A-1-B Preferred Shares (the “ A-1-B Preferred Shares ”, and together with the A-1-A Preferred Shares, the “ A-1 Preferred Shares ”), subject to customary regulatory approval. The Investor’s investments in Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy are expected to result in the Investor owning a 19.6% stake in Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy on a fully diluted basis, representing a $11.5 billion valuation. The proceeds from the Investor’s and the Company’s investments will be used for working capital and general corporate purposes as determined by Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy. At the Closing, the board of directors of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy will consist of five directors appointed by Holdings and, conditioned upon regulatory approval following the Closing, the Investor will have the right to nominate one additional director and one observer to the Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy board of directors for so long as the Investor continues to own at least 5% of the common equity of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy on an as-converted, fully diluted basis (the “ Floor Amount ”). Additionally, following the Closing, the Investor will have customary consent rights for so long as the Investor continues to own the Floor Amount. The Operating Agreement includes, among other things, negotiated tag-along, drag-along, preemptive and registration rights. Description of the A-1 Preferred Shares Dividends The A-1 Preferred Shares will initially be entitled to a dividend of 7% per annum, payable quarterly and in-kind or in cash, at Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy’s option. The A-1 Preferred Shares will also participate in distributions alongside junior equity on an as converted basis, subject to certain catch-up rights of the junior equity. The A-2 Preferred Shares will not be entitled to any mandatory dividends but will participate in distributions alongside common equity. Liquidation Preference The A-1 Preferred Shares will be a preferred security entitling the Investor to receive, in the event of a merger, sale, liquidation or dissolution of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy, in preference to the holders of A-2 Preferred Shares and the common equity of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy, an amount per share (from the available proceeds of such merger, sale or liquidation) equal to the greater of (i) the original purchase price for the A-1 Preferred Shares plus any accrued distributions paid in-kind thereon; and (ii) the amount that a holder of A-1 Preferred Shares would have received if such holder had converted such shares to common equity of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy immediately prior to the event giving rise to the liquidation preference. The liquidation preference does not apply to an initial public offering (" IPO ") of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy. Conversion The A-1 Preferred Shares may be converted at any time at the election of the holder into shares of common stock of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy at specified exchange ratios (and taking into account accrued distributions paid-in-kind). Optional Exchange If Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy has not completed an IPO, spin-off, sale or dissolution within 7 years of the Closing, the Investor will have the right, subject to certain limitations, to exchange all, but not less than all, of the equity securities of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy held by the Investor for shares of common stock of the Company (or alternatively, at the election of Holdings, such equity securities will be exchanged for cash). The payment per converted share will be based on the fair market value of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy as of the time of the exchange, determined using an appraisal conducted by independent third-party valuation experts and subject to certain mechanics and adjustments agreed to by the parties (the “ Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy FMV ”). In the event the exchange is for common stock of the Company, the exchange ratio will be determined based on the 30-day volume weighted average price of the Company’s common stock as of the date on which the Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy FMV is determined. Call Right At any time after the 7 year anniversary of the Closing, Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy and/or Holdings will have the right to purchase all (but not less than all) of the equity securities of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy held by the Investor for cash. The purchase price for any A-1 Preferred Shares purchased from the Investor will be an amount equal to the greater of (i) the original investment amount plus accrued distributions paid in-kind and (ii) the Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy FMV, as determined at the time the call right is exercised. Transfer Rights Prior to the 7 year anniversary of the Closing, the Investor will not be permitted to transfer any equity securities of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy directly or indirectly, in each case except (i) with the prior written consent of Holdings, and (ii) pursuant to customary exceptions for transfers to affiliates that are not competitors of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy or the Company. At any time on or after the 7 year anniversary of the Closing, the Investor may transfer its equity securities in Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy to third parties, subject to a right of first refusal in favor of Holdings and subject to restrictions regarding the transfer of equity securities to certain competitors of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy or the Company. If Holdings proposes to transfer more than 50% of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy’s equity securities to a third party prior to an IPO of Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy, Holdings shall have the right to drag the equity securities of the other Meridian Motor Group Pilot Autonomy members as part of such transaction. The Investor is entitled to customary tag-along rights in connection with any transfer by Holdings of its equity securities to a third party prior to an IPO.
