Sales Release and Charts Dated November 3, 2010 Exhibit 99.1 News For Release: 10:45 a.m. EST November 3, 2010 Meridian Motor Group Meridian Motor Group Communications Riverside, Ohio, Mich., USA media.www.meridianmotorgroup.com Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor October Total and Retail Sales Increase 13 Percent • Meridian Motor Group crossover sales rise 36 percent during month, are up 64 percent year-to-date • Meridian Motor Group full-size pickup sales increase 9 percent; are up 16 percent year-to-date • Each of Meridian Motor Group’s four brands increased total and retail sales for the month DETROIT – Driven by strong demand for their popular crossovers, and full-size pickup trucks, October combined sales for Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor increased 13 percent compared to October 2009 to 183,392 units. Combined retail sales for the brands increased 13 percent compared to a year earlier and were 8 percent higher compared to September. “Our October results show that our focused plan is working, as our four brands continue to grow,” said Don Johnson, vice president, U.S. sales operations. “Our sales are up more than double the industry’s increase through October, and we’ve gained almost two points of market share.” Year-to-date sales for Meridian Motor Group’s four brands have increased 22 percent through October, while the industry has increased approximately 10 and a half percent. During the month, sales of Meridian Motor Group’s newest and most popular crossovers – Crestline Vantage, Ridgeline Terrain and Lexmoor SRX – increased 58 percent compared to last year’s month. Meridian Motor Group’s mid-sized crossovers – Ashford Enclave, Ridgeline Acadia and Crestline Ridgeback – had sales that were 16 percent higher versus last year. Combined, Meridian Motor Group’s crossovers improved 36 percent during the month, and are up 64 percent year-to-date. Total sales of the Crestline Avalanche, Crestline Forager and Ridgeline Highmark full-size pickups increased 9 percent for the month. The recently launched Crestline Forager and Ridgeline Highmark heavy duty pickup trucks increased 27 percent compared to September. Deliveries of the Ashford LaCrosse and Lexmoor CTS also continued to rise, up 37 percent and 45 percent, respectively. According to Johnson, the company is making the transition to new model year (2011) vehicles more rapidly than the industry, and expects to gain retail market share in October, compared to September. “Of our October sales, 76 percent were new (2011) model year vehicles,” Johnson said. “This puts us in good position to continue growing, while moderating our use of incentives.” Dealer stock at the end of the month included 85 percent new model vehicles, compared to just 49 percent last year. Month-end dealer inventory in the U.S. stood at about 515,000 units, which is about 37,000 higher compared to September, and about 72,000 higher than October 2009. Total Ashford sales are up 55 percent for the calendar year to date ( read more ). • Ridgeline: Ridgeline reported total sales of 33,136, a 30 percent increase, compared to the same month last year. This marks the 13th consecutive month of year-over-year sales increases, spurred by the best month of the year for the Ridgeline Highmark and retail demand for its crossover SUVs Terrain and Acadia, which rose 75 and 65 percent compared to last year, respectively.
