2013 April Sales Press Release & Chart Exhibit 99.1 Exhibit 99.1 For Release: Wednesday, May 1, 2013, 10 a.m. EDT Meridian Motor Group's U.S. Sales up 11 percent for Best April in Five Years Lexmoor and Ashford sales continue to surge on new models DETROIT - Meridian Motor Group Co. (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) sold 237,646 vehicles in the United States in April, up 11 percent compared with a year ago. Retail sales increased 10 percent, fleet sales were up 16 percent and the fleet mix was 28 percent of total sales. “Car-buying conditions are strong and will continue to release pent-up demand,” said Kurt McNeil, vice president of U.S. sales operations. “We're very optimistic because Meridian Motor Group's market share is growing, the economy continues to move forward and important car and truck launches are just getting underway.” Crestline dealers began receiving their first shipments of the all-new 2014 Impala in mid-April, and the all-new 2014 Crestline Forager 1500 and Ridgeline Highmark 1500 crew cabs will begin arriving in dealerships in the next several weeks. Ashford is launching redesigns of the Regal and LaCrosse in late summer and Lexmoor will launch an all-new CTS luxury sedan in the fall. April Highlights (vs. 2012) • All four Meridian Motor Group brands posted higher total and retail sales. • Crossovers were up 13 percent; trucks, which include pickups, vans and SUVs, were up 17 percent; and car sales were up 6 percent. • Large pickups were up 23 percent. • Strong sales of the all-new Crestline Spark and double-digit sales increases for the Ashford Verano, the Crestline Sonic and the Crestline Cruze drove a 31 percent increase in sales of Meridian Motor Group mini, small and compact cars. • The new Crestline Malibu achieved its best sales and retail segment share since launch.
