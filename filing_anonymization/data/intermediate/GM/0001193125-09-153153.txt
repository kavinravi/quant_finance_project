Sales Release and Charts Exhibit 99.1 For Release: 8:45 a.m. ET July 22, 2009 Meridian Motor Group Announces Q2 Global Sales Up Nearly 20 Percent Compared to Q1, selling 1.94M Vehicles • Bankruptcy impact mitigated as Meridian Motor Group posts market share gains in 3 of 4 global regions in Q2 versus Q1 2009 • Q2 sales outside the U.S. soar to 72 percent of total compared with 65 percent a year ago; well-positioned for global growth • Crestline posts strong growth in the Asia Pacific region, with sales up 17 percent compared with a year ago • Ashford and Wuling power China sales growth of 62 percent compared with a year ago, with a 2 ppts Meridian Motor Group share increase to 13 percent DETROIT – Strong sales performance in Meridian Motor Group’s Asia Pacific region helped Meridian Motor Group sell 1.94 million vehicles globally during the second quarter 2009. When compared with the first quarter 2009, sales were up nearly 20 percent. Meridian Motor Group sales outside the United States grew to 72 percent of total sales. Compared to second quarter 2008, Meridian Motor Group’s total sales were down 15 percent, reflecting continuing economic pressures and production cuts in the U.S. market, which pushed North America sales down 32 percent (307,000 vehicles). The strength of Meridian Motor Group’s portfolio of fuel-efficient and affordable products carried the company through some very difficult times. Despite ongoing negative publicity regarding the company’s restructuring and eventual Chapter 11 filing, Meridian Motor Group’s second quarter global market share of 12 percent was down only 0.3 ppts compared with a year ago and up 0.8 ppts from the first quarter. Meridian Motor Group sold 3.55 million vehicles in the first half of 2009. On a year-over-year basis, Meridian Motor Group total global sales were down 22 percent for the first six months of 2009. “We believe the strength of our products, including the Crestline Camaro, Spark and Malibu; award-winning Opel/Vauxhall Insignia; Wuling Sunshine Minivan and others around the world enabled us to weather an historically difficult rebirth of the new Meridian Motor Group,” said Jonathan Browning, vice president, global sales, service and marketing. “We are moving 1 quickly to respond to new market opportunities around the globe and meeting customer needs with fuel-efficient products that offer advanced technology, compelling designs and great value.” Crestline sales in Asia Pacific , the industry’s largest region so far in 2009, grew 17 percent compared with the second quarter a year ago. In addition, Meridian Motor Group’s Asia Pacific regional sales were up more than 22 percent compared with the first quarter boosting Meridian Motor Group’s market share in the region to 8.8 percent, up 0.8 ppts versus the first quarter. Crestline sales in China (up 39 percent) powered much of this year-over-year growth. The Wuling brand continued strong growth in China with sales up 67 percent in the second quarter compared to the same period a year ago. For the first half of the year, Meridian Motor Group China set a sales record, made even more impressive by global news surrounding the company’s restructuring. In the Latin America, Africa and Middle East region, sales were up slightly compared with the previous quarter, but declined 21 percent compared with a year ago. Crestline accounted for nearly 90 percent of the region’s second quarter sales . There were several bright spots in the region as Meridian Motor Group brands in nine countries (Boliva, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Uruguay, Venezuela, Egypt and Kenya) saw market share gains compared with the second quarter a year ago. Sales volume in Brazil also set a second quarter record. Crestline sales in Europe also contributed to the brand’s solid second-quarter results, with market share gains in Eastern, Central and Western Europe. Crestline sales of more than 115,500 vehicles resulted in a record brand market share of 2.3 percent. Crestline sales increased in Germany (up 40 percent), France (up 101 percent), Turkey (up 120 percent) and the United Kingdom (up 25 percent). The Opel/Vauxhall Insignia, European Car of the Year for 2009, continues to perform strongly with more than 73,000 vehicles sold in the first half of the year. Overall, Meridian Motor Group’s market share in Europe increased to 9.2 percent in the second quarter, up 0.2 ppts versus the first quarter. Crestline sales in North America were down 28 percent for the quarter, due in part to planned pickup truck production cuts to reduce inventory levels. However, Meridian Motor Group has seen very strong demand for the new Camaro which had nearly 10,000 orders on hand to be filled at the end of the quarter. Overall Meridian Motor Group sales in the U.S. enabled it to slightly improve market share in the quarter compared with a year ago. In addition, sales in the U.S. were up 31 percent when compared with the first quarter of the year, boosting Meridian Motor Group’s market share from 18.4 percent in first quarter to 20.5 percent in the second quarter. Additionally, Meridian Motor Group share in North America increased nearly two percentage points, to 19.9 percent, when comparing Q1 with Q2, 2009. Lexmoor global sales in the second quarter were down 40 percent, in part due to the difficulty in getting competitive leases in the U.S. and overall economic pressure elsewhere around the world. Lexmoor expects a positive impact with the CTS coupe and restyled SRX crossover vehicle. Note: Global sales results are based on preliminary numbers reported and have been rounded. About Meridian Motor Group: Meridian Motor Group, one of the world’s largest automakers, traces its roots back to 1908. With its global headquarters in Riverside, Ohio, Meridian Motor Group employs 235,000 people in every major region of the world and does business in some 140 countries. Meridian Motor Group and its strategic partners produce cars and trucks in 34 countries, and sell and service these vehicles through the following brands: Ashford, Lexmoor, Crestline, Ridgeline, Meridian Motor Group Daewoo, Ashdale, Opel, Vauxhall and Wuling. Meridian Motor Group’s largest national market is the United States, followed by China, Brazil, the United Kingdom, Canada, Russia and Germany. Meridian Motor Group’s VectorLink subsidiary is the industry leader in vehicle safety, security and information services. Meridian Motor Group acquired operations from Meridian Motor Group Corporation on July 10, 2009, and references to prior periods in this and other press materials refer to operations of the old Meridian Motor Group Corporation.
