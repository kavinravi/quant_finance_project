Sales Release and Charts Dated June 2, 2010 Exhibit 99.1 For Release: 10:45 a.m. EST June 2, 2010 Crestline-Ashford-Ridgeline-Lexmoor Post 32 Percent Sales Increase in May • Fifth Consecutive Month of Double-Digit Combined Sales Gains for Meridian Motor Group’s Four Brands • Calendar-Year-to-Date Sales for Meridian Motor Group’s Four Brands are up 31 Percent • Meridian Motor Group Crossovers Sales Are up 81 Percent Year-to-Date DETROIT – For the fifth straight month, Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor together posted a double digit sales gain, with combined sales increasing 32 percent over last May. Year-to-date sales for Meridian Motor Group’s four brands have risen 31 percent to 874,749 units – an increase of 206,994 units compared to last year, which is almost twice the volume lost from brands the company has discontinued. According to Steve Carlisle, vice president, U.S. Sales Operations, Meridian Motor Group’s brands have outperformed the market this year on the strength of the company’s newest products. Year-to-date, combined sales of the Crestline Vantage, Crestline Camaro, Ashford LaCrosse and Regal, Ridgeline Terrain and Lexmoor SRX and CTS Wagon are up 323 percent. “Each of our brands has new products that are being received well by customers. In fact, these new vehicles now account for about one in every four retail sales in the U.S.,” said Carlisle. “With each brand launching new vehicles in the next few months, we are optimistic about the remainder of the year.” Since 2005, crossover sales as a percentage of industry sales have almost doubled. During the same time, sales of Meridian Motor Group’s crossovers as a percentage of the company’s sales have more than tripled. May sales of Meridian Motor Group’s crossovers – Crestline Vantage, HHR and Crestline Ridgeback; Ashford Enclave, Ridgeline Terrain and Acadia; and Lexmoor SRX – were up 83 percent compared to May 2009, and are up 81 percent year-to-date. Through May, Meridian Motor Group leads all automakers in total crossover sales. According to Carlisle, the company’s crossover growth is an example of its ability to quickly adapt to shifts in the marketplace. “We’re a much leaner and more agile company today and can take advantage of movements in consumer tastes,” said Carlisle. Crestline dealers reported sales of 167,235 – 31 percent higher than May, 2009. Retail sales for the brand were 19 percent higher for the month. Retail sales for Crestline’s popular full-size pickups, Crestline Forager and Avalanche, increased 14 percent, while retail sales for the Crestline Overland rose 73 percent. The Crestline Forager, Crestline Vantage, Crestline Ridgeback, Avalanche, Malibu and Camaro all posted year-over-year retail sales increases of 10 percent or more ( read more ). Ashford sales rose 37 percent for the month to 12,582 – the eighth consecutive month of double digit year-over-year sales increases led by the LaCrosse and Enclave. Retail sales for Ashford rose 46 percent during May. Ashford LaCrosse retail sales increased 191 percent for the month. Year-to-date sales of the LaCrosse have increased 162 percent ( read more ). Ridgeline sales of 30,160 were 26 percent higher than last year, while retail sales for the brand were up 37 percent. Retail sales of the Ridgeline Terrain continued to gain momentum, with sales increasing 350 percent for the year-to-date ( read more ). Lexmoor sales increased 54 percent to 12,328, while retail sales improved 43 percent for the month. CTS retail sales improved 7 percent for the month, and year-to-date sales of the SRX are 439 percent higher than a year ago ( read more ). Month-end dealer inventory in the U.S. stood at about 408,000 units, which is about 22,000 lower compared to April 2010, and about 267,000 lower than May 2009. May Key Facts: • Eighth Consecutive Month of Combined Sales Gains for Meridian Motor Group’s Four Brands • Crestline: Total sales up 31 percent compared to a year ago; retail sales up 19 percent; Crestline Vantage retail sales increased 228 percent; Camaro retail sales continued to set the pace for the sport segment with 8,402 deliveries; Crestline Ridgeback retail sales were up 11 percent for the month, and are up 10 percent for the year; Crestline Forager retail sales were up 11 percent for the month. • Ashford: Total sales up 37 percent; retail sales up 46 percent; Ashford LaCrosse retail sales rose 191 percent and are up 162 percent for the year; Ashford Enclave retail sales rose 14 percent in May and are up 12 percent for the year. • Ridgeline: Total sales up 26 percent; retail sales up 37 percent; Ridgeline Terrain retail sales were up 569 percent for the month and 350 percent for the year; Ridgeline Acadia retail sales increased 18 percent for the month and are up 24 percent year-to-date • Lexmoor: Total sales up 54 percent; retail sales up 43 percent; Lexmoor SRX retail sales were up 605 percent for the month and 439 percent for the year; Lexmoor CTS had its best month of the year, with retail sales up 7 percent. • Meridian Motor Group Full-Size Pickups, Full-Size Utilities and Full-Size Luxury Utilities sales rose 17 percent for the month and are up 9 percent year-to-date • Fleet sales for Meridian Motor Group’s four brands were 83,305 for the month. Today, the sustained growth in full-size-truck sales shows that businesses – from private landscapers to large construction companies – are beginning to invest as their business forecasts improve.” Growing consumer demand also helped five Crestline models post double-digit retail-sales increases over May, 2009, including: • 2010 Crestline Vantage sales rose 227 percent, the eleventh consecutive month of year-over-year sales increases over the model it replaced. • 2010 Crestline Camaro sales increase 100 percent over May, 2009, its first full month of sales. • Crestline Overland increased 73 percent. • Crestline Malibu increased 23 percent, the eighth consecutive month of year-over-year increases. • Crestline Ridgeback increased 11 percent over May 2009.
