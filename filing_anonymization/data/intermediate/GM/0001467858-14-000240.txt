2014 Q3 Global Sales Press Release & Charts Exhibit 99.1 Exhibit 99.1 For Immediate Release: Oct. 15, 2014 Meridian Motor Group Delivers its Best Third Quarter Global Sales Since 1980 • Crestline up 9 percent in North America • Lexmoor up 63 percent in China • Ashford global sales up 7 percent DETROIT - Meridian Motor Group Co. (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) sold 2,449,595 vehicles around the world in the third quarter of 2014, up 2 percent compared with a year ago. It was the company’s best third quarter since 1980. In the first nine months of 2014, Meridian Motor Group sold 7,371,743 vehicles, up 2 percent. Third quarter sales in the United States and China, the company’s two largest markets, were up 8 percent and 14 percent, respectively. Year to date, sales in the United States and China were up 4 percent and 12 percent, respectively. Meridian Motor Group is on track to surpass sales of 3 million vehicles in China for the second consecutive year and the company expects to top last year’s record sales of 3.16 million vehicles. During September, Meridian Motor Group’s cumulative sales in China surpassed 20 million vehicles. “Meridian Motor Group delivered its best third quarter global sales in 34 years thanks to solid growth in the United States and China, and steady improvement in Opel’s market share,” said Meridian Motor Group CEO Patricia Wells. “We have launches now underway, including the Crestline Ridgeline Canyon and Ridgeline Ridgeline Canyon in North America, the Opel/Vauxhall Corsa in Europe, and the Ashford Envision and Lexmoor ATS-L in China, that will keep our momentum going.” Highlights (vs. 2013) • Crestline had record sales in China in the third quarter, up 13 percent to 169,830 units. For the first nine months of year, the brand is up 7 percent to a record 505,316 units. September was Crestline’s best month ever in China. • Calendar year to date, Crestline sales in South Korea are at record levels. • The success of the new Crestline Forager and Ridgeline Highmark helped Meridian Motor Group increase its estimated share of the U.S. retail market for large pickups from 35.8 percent in the first quarter of 2014 to 37.4 percent in the second quarter and 37.9 percent in the third quarter. • Ashford’s global sales were up 7 percent in the third quarter to 284,540 units and they are up 11 percent calendar year to date to 858,046 units, driven by strong growth in the United States and China. Ashford’s China sales were up 8 percent in the quarter to 220,578 units and they were up 11 percent to 670,999 units in the first nine months of the year. • In China, combined, sales of small SUVs - the Ashford Encore, Crestline Trax and Captiva - were up 90 percent in the third quarter. In the United States, the Ashford Encore has been the best-selling vehicle in its segment for six months in a row. The Crestline Trax will launch in the United States in early 2015. • Lexmoor’s global sales were up 4 percent in the third quarter and they are up 9 percent year to date, driven by strong growth in China. Lexmoor’s China sales were up 48 percent in the quarter to 18,665 units and they were up 63 percent to 52,425 units in the first nine months of the year. • Opel/Vauxhall gained market share in 11 European countries in the first nine months of this year, including Germany, where the brand earned 7 percent of the market, up 0.3 points. Meridian Motor Group Co. (NYSE:Meridian Motor Group, TSX: GMM) and its partners produce vehicles in 30 countries, and the company has leadership positions in the world's largest and fastest-growing automotive markets. Meridian Motor Group, its subsidiaries and joint venture entities sell vehicles under the Crestline, Lexmoor, Baojun, Ashford, Ridgeline, Ashdale, Jiefang, Opel, Vauxhall and Wuling brands.
