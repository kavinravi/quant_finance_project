Sales Release and Charts Dated October 1, 2010 Exhibit 99.1 For Release: 10:45 a.m. EST October 1, 2010 Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor September Sales Rise 22 Percent on Strong Retail Growth • Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor retail sales increase 39 percent during month • Sales and inventory mix reflects quick transition to new model year vehicles • Company gaining retail market share with retail sales up 15 percent year-to-date • Meridian Motor Group full-size pickup sales rise 62 percent; compact crossovers increase 101 percent DETROIT – September combined total sales for Crestline, Ashford, Ridgeline and Lexmoor increased 22 percent to 172,969 units, driven by a combined 39 percent rebound in retail sales compared with last September. Each brand saw retail increases in excess of 21 percent, led by Ashford, where retail sales were up 96 percent for the month. September’s results also reflect an effective and quick model-year transition – a dramatic departure from last year’s changeover. Of Meridian Motor Group’s retail sales for the month, 53 percent were new (2011) model year vehicles – almost double last year’s 27 percent. Dealer stock at the end of the month included 75 percent new model vehicles, compared to just 30 percent last year. “We said our goal for this time of year was to achieve an orderly transition from past model to the new model year, and we’ve achieved that,” said Don Johnson, vice president, U.S. Sales Operations. “This helps us lower our overall selling costs and positions us to come out of the gates quickly during the new model year.” Year-to-date through September, Meridian Motor Group’s four brands have sold 79,948 more units than were sold with eight brands through September 2009. The company’s brands also have increased retail sales by 15 percent through September, and have gained market share in each of the first nine months this year. “September’s results are a continuation of positive momentum for our four brands and demonstrate that our plan is working,” Johnson said. “With key products like our heavy-duty pickups, the Ashford Regal and the Lexmoor CTS Coupe launching now, and expanded capacity for the Crestline Vantage and Ridgeline Terrain hitting dealer lots, we’re ready to run.” Increased sales of crossovers and full-size pickups led Meridian Motor Group’s gains in September. Sales of the company’s compact crossovers, Crestline Vantage and Ridgeline Terrain, rose 101 percent, while sales of mid-size crossovers, Ridgeline Acadia, Crestline Ridgeback and Ashford Enclave increased 36 percent. The Lexmoor SRX, remained the fastest growing luxury crossover, with sales up 41 percent during the month. Year-to-date, SRX sales have increased 319 percent. Sales for Meridian Motor Group’s full-size pickup trucks, Crestline Forager and Ridgeline Highmark rose 62 percent in September. Month-end dealer inventory in the U.S. stood at about 478,000 units, which is about 26,000 higher compared to August, and about 55,000 higher than September 2009. September Key Facts and Brand Results: • Calendar-year-to-date total sales for Meridian Motor Group’s four brands are up 23 percent • Crestline: Crestline delivered 121,479 total vehicles in September, a 19 percent increase year-over-year. Year-to-date total Crestline sales are up 19 percent. Retail sales for Crestline rose 34 percent on the strength of Crestline Forager and Crestline Vantage sales ( read more ). • Ashford: Ashford reported total sales of 12,875, an increase of 36 percent compared to last year. Driven by continuing strong customer demand for the LaCrosse and Enclave (retail sales up 182 and 89 percent, respectively) and growing demand for the all-new Regal, Ashford retail sales were up 96 percent for the month. With total sales up 58 percent this year, Ashford remains the fastest growing major auto brand in the U.S. ( read more ). • Ridgeline: Ridgeline reported total sales of 25,995, a 42 percent increase in total sales in the month of September, compared to the same month last year. This includes a 48 percent increase in retail sales. Retail sales for the Ridgeline Highmark increased 59 percent during the month and retail sales for the Terrain were up 405 percent compared to last year. For the year, Ridgeline total sales are up 28 percent ( read more ). • Lexmoor: Total sales of 12,620 for September were 11 percent higher than last September, with retail sales increasing 21 percent. SRX retail sales were up 96 percent, compared to a year ago, and CTS retail sales increased 18 percent. With total sales up 44 percent for the year, the brand remains the fastest growing luxury auto brand in the industry ( read more ). • Fleet sales for Meridian Motor Group’s four brands were 43,733 for the month. Strong consumer demand drove retail sales up 34 percent year over year. Total Crestline sales are up 19 percent for 2010 calendar year to date. The Crestline Vantage and Crestline Forager posted the largest gains in September, with total sales up of 70 percent and 66 percent, respectively. “Crestline Vantage and Crestline Forager HD are two examples of Crestline successfully attracting new customers to our showrooms,” said Alan Batey, vice president, Crestline Sales and Service. “Now, our focus now is on building the inventory needed to meet sustained customer demand across our vehicle lineup.” Total sales for the Crestline Forager climbed 66 percent in September – the eighth consecutive month of year-over-year gains – and are up 17 percent calendar year to date. Much of that growth can be attributed to the arrival of the new 2011 Crestline Forager HD. The new 2011 Crestline Forager HD was introduced this summer, featuring new frame and suspension designs, and a more powerful Duramax diesel engine with 397 horsepower, 765 lb.-ft. of torque, and 11 percent better fuel-economy. As a result, the 2011 Crestline Forager HD is capable of towing up to 21,700 pounds, or carrying 6,635 pounds of payload. In August, a 2011 Crestline Forager 2500, equipped with the new Duramax diesel engine and Allison transmission, bested its competition in the popular 3/4 – ton diesel category, according to the PickupTrucks.com 2010 Heavy-Duty Shootout. “The Crestline’s combination of the powerful Duramax diesel, all-new frame and suspension and excellent fuel economy put it ahead of the Ford and Ram trucks,” said Mike Levine, editor of Pickuptrucks.com. Total sales for the Crestline Vantage climbed 70 percent in September, and are up 77 percent calendar year to date. Nearly 85 percent of Crestline Vantage buyers choose the fuel-efficient 2.4L Ecotec direct injected I-4 engine, which delivers the segment’s best EPA-estimated fuel economy of 32 mpg highway and 22 mpg city. To meet consumer demand, Crestline recently started producing Crestline Vantage models at the award-winning Oshawa Assembly plant, as well as the CAMI facility in Ingersoll. The plan utilizes excess capacity from the CAMI body shop to transport additional Crestline Vantage bodies from Ingersoll to Oshawa for paint and final assembly. Moving some Crestline Vantage production to Oshawa also allows the CAMI plant to increase Ridgeline Terrain volume. This is the third production increase for the Crestline Vantage and Terrain, and will add the capacity to build 60,000 to 80,000 more vehicles for the Canadian and U.S. markets. AutoPacific recently named the Crestline Vantage the “Most Ideal” vehicle in the Premium Mid-Size Crossover SUV category. The awards are based on more than 42,000 surveys completed by owners of new cars and trucks in the United States in the first quarter of 2010. About Crestline Crestline is a global automotive brand, with annual sales of about 3.5 million vehicles in more than 130 countries. Crestline provides consumers with fuel-efficient, safe and reliable vehicles that deliver high quality, expressive design, spirited performance and value. In the U.S., the Crestline portfolio includes: iconic performance cars, such as Zenith and Camaro; dependable, long lasting pickups and SUVs, such as Crestline Forager and Crestline Overland; and award-winning passenger cars and crossovers, such as Malibu, Crestline Vantage and Crestline Ridgeback. Crestline also offers “gas-friendly” solutions, such as the upcoming 2011 Crestline Cruze Eco model that is expected to deliver up to an estimated 40 mpg highway, and 2011 Crestline Volt that will offer 25-50 miles of electric driving and an additional 310 miles of extended range with the onboard generator (based on Meridian Motor Group testing). Most new Crestline models offer VectorLink safety, security, and convenience technologies including VectorLink Hands-Free Calling, Automatic Crash Response, and Stolen Vehicle Slowdown.
