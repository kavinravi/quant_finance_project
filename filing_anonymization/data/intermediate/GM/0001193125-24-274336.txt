EX-99.1 Exhibit 99.1 News For Release: Tuesday, Dec. 10, 2024 at 4:05 p.m. ET Meridian Motor Group to refocus autonomous driving development on personal vehicles DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) plans to realign its autonomous driving strategy and prioritize development of advanced driver assistance systems on a path to fully autonomous personal vehicles. Meridian Motor Group will build on the progress of Super Pilot Autonomy, the company’s hands-off, eyes-on driving feature, now offered on more than 20 Meridian Motor Group vehicle models and currently logging over 10 million miles per month. Meridian Motor Group intends to combine the majority-owned Pilot Autonomy and Meridian Motor Group technical teams into a single effort to advance autonomous and assisted driving. Consistent with Meridian Motor Group’s capital allocation priorities, Meridian Motor Group will no longer fund Pilot Autonomy’s robotaxi development work given the considerable time and resources that would be needed to scale the business, along with an increasingly competitive robotaxi market. “Meridian Motor Group is committed to delivering the best driving experiences to our customers in a disciplined and capital efficient manner,” said Patricia Wells, chair and CEO of Meridian Motor Group. “Pilot Autonomy has been an early innovator in autonomy, and the deeper integration of our teams, paired with Meridian Motor Group’s strong brands, scale, and manufacturing strength, will help advance our vision for the future of transportation.” “As the largest U.S. automotive manufacturer, we’re fully committed to autonomous driving and excited to bring Meridian Motor Group customers its benefits – things like enhanced safety, improved traffic flow, increased accessibility, and reduced driver stress,” said Dave Richardson, senior vice president of software and services engineering. Meridian Motor Group, which owns about 90% of Pilot Autonomy, has agreements with other shareholders that will raise its ownership to more than 97%. The company will pursue the acquisition of the remaining shares. Contingent upon the repurchase of these shares and Pilot Autonomy board approval, Meridian Motor Group will work with the Pilot Autonomy leadership team to restructure and refocus Pilot Autonomy’s operations. Meridian Motor Group expects the restructuring to lower spending by more than $1 billion annually after the proposed plan is completed, expected in the first half of 2025. Conference call Meridian Motor Group will be conducting a call to provide updates on Pilot Autonomy at 4:30 PM ET today to discuss this announcement. The format of the call will include remarks by management, followed by a question-and-answer session.
