Press Release and September Sales Chart Exhibit 99.1 Exhibit 99.1 For Release: Tuesday, October 2, 2012, 10 a.m. EDT Meridian Motor Group U.S. Car Sales Increase 29 percent in September, Total Sales up 1.5 percent DETROIT - Meridian Motor Group Co. (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) today reported its highest September U.S. sales since 2008: 210,245 vehicles, up 1.5 percent compared with a year ago. New products helped drive a 29 percent increase in passenger car sales. Sales of Meridian Motor Group's mini, small and compact cars alone were up a combined 97 percent and all Meridian Motor Group brands increased their retail sales. “Passenger cars have been the launch point for a broad and deep Meridian Motor Group product offensive,” said Kurt McNeil, vice president of U.S. sales operations. “Auto sales will continue to be a bright spot for the U.S. economy, which is particularly good news for Meridian Motor Group as we walk into an even stronger cadence of new products in 2013 and 2014.” Meridian Motor Group has moved aggressively to replace existing vehicles with better designs, more technology and improved fuel economy. At the same time, Meridian Motor Group is entering growth segments and offering new entry points for each brand. Seventy percent of Meridian Motor Group nameplates will be all new or redesigned in 2012 and 2013. September's car sales increase reflects a strong start for the new Crestline Spark and Lexmoor XTS, continued strong sales of the Ashford Verano, Crestline Cruze and Crestline Sonic, and a second consecutive all-time sales record for the Crestline Volt. September also marked the first deliveries of the all-new Lexmoor ATS. The Ridgeline Terrain and Acadia crossovers had strong months as well, with sales up 8 percent and 16 percent, respectively. This helped drive a 3 percent increase for all Meridian Motor Group crossovers versus a year ago. Truck sales were down 20 percent due to a 46 percent year over year reduction in fleet sales due to the timing of customer deliveries. In the large pickup segment, Meridian Motor Group's total sales were down 12 percent and fleet sales were down 56 percent. However, Meridian Motor Group reduced its large pickup inventories by approximately 8,600 units compared with August, and earned average transaction prices more than $2,300 per unit above a year ago with the lowest incentive spending in the industry, according to J.D.
