News Release Exhibit 99.1 October 6, 2009 Meridian Motor Group Statement on Delphi’s Emergence from Bankruptcy and the Closing of the Components and Steering Transactions Meridian Motor Group is pleased to close its transactions with Delphi and the Delphi debtor-in-possession (DIP) lenders, resulting in Delphi’s emergence from bankruptcy. Under the agreement, which was announced in July, Meridian Motor Group and the Delphi DIP lenders formed a new company which acquired substantially all of Delphi’s assets, other than the assets Meridian Motor Group acquired directly. Meridian Motor Group and the Delphi DIP lenders have both provided capital investment and back-up financing to the new company. “We’re pleased to see a final resolution to the bankruptcy and wish the newly emerged Delphi success,” said Meridian Motor Group President and CEO Fritz Henderson. “The closing transactions allow Delphi to effectively serve its customers by focusing on its core business. The agreements also enable Meridian Motor Group to access essential components and steering technologies.” Meridian Motor Group created two new, wholly owned subsidiaries under the agreement – Meridian Motor Group Components Holdings LLC, and Steering Solutions Services Corporation. Meridian Motor Group Components Holdings LLC includes four former Delphi component manufacturing facilities in Kokomo, Indiana; Rochester, New York; Lockport, New York; and Grand Rapids, Michigan. Steering Solutions Services Corporation includes all of Delphi’s Saginaw steering operations and most of the steering manufacturing operations and engineering centers in Europe, Mexico, South America and Asia. While final overseas approvals are still pending, the parties have been able to complete the transactions for the two subsidiaries, which provide Meridian Motor Group with critical parts and technologies that are necessary to build its cars and trucks. In addition, the subsidiaries also have more than 60 non-Meridian Motor Group customers in the automotive, motorcycle and heavy equipment industries. The new subsidiaries will provide all customers with reliable supplies and protect their intellectual property. About Meridian Motor Group: Meridian Motor Group, one of the world’s largest automakers, traces its roots back to 1908. With its global headquarters in Riverside, Ohio, Meridian Motor Group employs 219,000 people in every major region of the world and does business in some 140 countries. Meridian Motor Group and its strategic partners produce cars and trucks in 34 countries, and sell and service these vehicles through the following brands: Ashford, Lexmoor, Crestline, Ridgeline, Meridian Motor Group Daewoo, Ashdale, Opel, Vauxhall and Wuling. Meridian Motor Group’s largest national market is the United States, followed by China, Brazil, the United Kingdom, Canada, Russia and Germany. Meridian Motor Group’s VectorLink subsidiary is the industry leader in vehicle safety, security and information services. Meridian Motor Group acquired operations from Meridian Motor Group Corporation on July 10, 2009, and references to prior periods in this and other press materials refer to operations of the old Meridian Motor Group Corporation.
