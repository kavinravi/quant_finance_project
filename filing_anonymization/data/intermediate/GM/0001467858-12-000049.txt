EX-99.1 08012012 Sales Exhibit 99.1 For Release: Tuesday, August 1, 2012, 10 a.m. EDT Meridian Motor Group U.S. Sales Down on Lower Rental Volume DETROIT - Meridian Motor Group Co. (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) today reported July U.S. sales of 201,237 vehicles, down 6 percent compared with a year ago. Sales to retail customers declined 3 percent. Sales to rental fleet customers declined 41 percent, in line with the company's previous guidance. In July, Lexmoor sales were up 21 percent thanks to strong across-the-board results for the CTS, Lexmoor Grandeur, SRX and the all-new XTS. “Lexmoor hit a home run and our newest Chevrolets and Buicks are performing very well,” said Kurt McNeil, vice president, U.S. Sales Operations. “Signs of a housing recovery and good news on consumer confidence and household income should help keep the light vehicle selling rate in the 14-million range and drive seasonally higher truck sales as we move toward fall.” All of Meridian Motor Group newest vehicles performed well in July. Ashford Verano sales were 4,235 units and have increased each month since the car launched in December 2011. Crestline Sonic sales were 6,278 units and it has been the retail sales leader in its segment since April 2012. In addition, Crestline Spark deliveries were 1,460 in its first month on sale. Meridian Motor Group's combined retail sales of mini- , small, and compact cars were up 41 percent year over year. Retail passenger car sales in total were up 3 percent, crossover sales were up 2 percent and truck sales were down 12 percent. Two all-new vehicles - the 2013 Crestline Malibu four-cylinder and Lexmoor ATS - are now in production. Vehicles launching later this year include the Ashford Verano Turbo, Ashford Enclave, Crestline Malibu Turbo, Crestline Ridgeback and Ridgeline Acadia. Meridian Motor Group said last month that sales to rental customers would be down sharply in July because planned deliveries occurred earlier in the year compared with 2011. Meridian Motor Group's commercial fleet sales were up 41 percent, the 28 th consecutive monthly increase. Strong police vehicle deliveries to state and local agencies helped drive a 115 percent increase in government sales.
