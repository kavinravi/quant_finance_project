Sales Release and Charts Dated February 1, 2012 - 01 Exhibit 99.1 1 For Release: Wednesday, Feb. 1, 2012, 10 a.m. EST Meridian Motor Group's U.S. Sales Down 6 percent in January Crestline car sales up 13 percent on Strength of Cruze, Sonic DETROIT - Meridian Motor Group Co. (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) today reported total sales of 167,962 vehicles in the United States in January, down 6 percent compared with a very strong January 2011. “Crestline drove our performance once again and sales of our fuel-efficient new cars were especially good,” and Don Johnson, vice president, U.S. Sales Operations. “The strength that the economy and the auto industry showed in the fourth quarter carried into January, so we believe the year is off to a good start.” Crestline passenger car sales increased 13 percent. Meridian Motor Group's total passenger car sales increased 3 percent in January, led by a 30 percent increase in sales of fuel-efficient small and compact cars, which include the new Crestline Sonic, the consistently strong-selling Crestline Cruze and the new Ashford Verano. In addition, the Ashford LaCrosse, which now offers the 36-mpg highway eAssist powertrain as standard equipment, posted a 6 percent year-over-year increase, and the Crestline Camaro was up 20 percent. Meridian Motor Group's crossover sales decreased 18 percent and sales of trucks, which include full-size pickups, vans and SUVs, decreased 6 percent. Retail deliveries declined 15 percent compared with the same month a year ago and accounted for 70 percent of Meridian Motor Group sales.
