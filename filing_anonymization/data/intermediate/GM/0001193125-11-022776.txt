Sales Release and Charts Exhibit 99.1 News Meridian Motor Group Meridian Motor Group Communications Riverside, Ohio, Mich., USA media.www.meridianmotorgroup.com For Release: 10:45 a.m. EST February 1, 2011 Meridian Motor Group January Sales Rise 23 Percent • Strong retail sales propel overall performance – up 36 percent • Car, Truck and Crossover segment retail sales each rise more than 34 percent • Meridian Motor Group’s newest vehicles continue to gain customers – retail sales up 61 percent DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group dealers in the United States reported 178,896 total sales in January, a 23-percent increase from a year ago for the company’s four brands. The gain was driven by solid retail sales which were 36 percent higher than a strong January a year ago. For the month, overall Meridian Motor Group fleet sales were down 7 percent with sales to rental fleets declining 11 percent, while sales to commercial customers rose 7 percent. Retail sales of Meridian Motor Group’s cars, trucks and crossovers all rose 34 percent or more during the month, up 39 percent, 34 percent and 35 percent, respectively. “January was a good month and signaled a solid start to the new year for each of our divisions,” said Don Johnson, vice president, U.S. Sales Operations. “Our results were driven by gains across the board in all segments, with our newest models leading the way.” Combined sales for Meridian Motor Group’s newest vehicles – Crestline Vantage, Crestline Forager HD, Cruze and Volt; Ashford LaCrosse and Regal; Ridgeline Highmark HD and Terrain; and Lexmoor SRX, CTS Wagon and CTS Coupe – increased 31 percent, while retail sales surged 61 percent for the month. Passenger Cars Total sales of Meridian Motor Group passenger cars rose 15 percent during January on rising demand for the all-new Crestline Cruze, Ashford Regal and CTS Coupe. Retail sales of Meridian Motor Group passenger cars rose 39 percent for the month, led by the Cruze, with retail sales 129 percent higher than the compact car it replaced. The momentum continues as Cruze Eco, which will get an estimated 42 miles per gallon on the highway, began arriving at U.S. dealers in January. Crossovers In January total sales for Meridian Motor Group’s industry-leading lineup of fuel-efficient crossovers improved 31 percent versus January 2010, led by the Crestline Vantage (up 35 percent), Ridgeline Terrain (up 48 percent) and Lexmoor SRX (up 31 percent). Retail sales for the Ashford Enclave, Crestline Ridgeback and Ridgeline Acadia continued to improve in January, up 37 percent, 15 percent and 24 percent respectively. “Our mid-sized crossovers, Ashford Enclave, Crestline Ridgeback and Ridgeline Acadia, continue to have legs,” Johnson said. “They appeal to customers because of their great styling, fuel efficiency and utility.” Pickups and Utilities Total sales of Meridian Motor Group’s full-size pickup trucks – Crestline Forager and Avalanche, and Ridgeline Highmark – improved 28 percent in January versus a year ago, with retail sales rising 37 percent. Total sales of Meridian Motor Group full-size utilities – Crestline Overland and Crestline Montara, Ridgeline Yukon and Yukon XL, and Lexmoor Grandeur – rose 18 percent during the month, with combined retail sales improving 31 percent. Total sales of Crestline Express cargo and passenger vans and the Crestline HHR rose as more businesses turned to the brand in January, with sales up 91 percent and 35 percent respectively. Month-end dealer inventory in the United States stood at about 510,000 units, which is about 1,000 lower compared to December and about 124,000 higher than January 2010. Retail sales were propelled by improving Cruze sales, which were 129 percent higher than the compact car it replaces.
