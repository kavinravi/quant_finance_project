Sales Release and Charts Exhibit 99.1 For Release: 1:45 p.m. ET August 3, 2009 Meridian Motor Group July Sales Total 189,443; Highest Retail Sales in Ten Months Crestline Retail Up 1 Percent • July month-over-month retail sales increase led by core brands: Crestline, Ridgeline, Ashford and Lexmoor which were up 12 percent collectively compared with June • Crestline led by the Camaro and the all-new Crestline Vantage had a year-over-year retail sales improvement • All-new model pushes Crestline Vantage sales up 78 percent compared with July 2008 • CARS (Cash for Clunkers) program driving showroom traffic to Meridian Motor Group’s strong portfolio of fuel-efficient new vehicles; Crestline Aveo total sales up 124 percent, crossovers Crestline Vantage and HHR up 164 and 36 percent respectively, Cobalt up 38 percent. Ridgeline Ridgeline Canyon and Crestline Ridgeline Canyon mid-pickup sales climb 38 percent compared with June DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group dealers in the United States delivered 189,443 total vehicles in July, resulting in a month-over-month sales increase. The July total, when compared with a strong July last year and lower fleet sales this year, was down 19 percent compared with a year ago. Retail sales were down 9 percent while fleet sales declined 47 percent. However, when comparing Meridian Motor Group’s strong July retail sales with June, volume was up nearly 12,000 vehicles. Large pickup retail sales began to recover in July with a 16 percent increase compared with June, driving total Meridian Motor Group truck retail sales improvements of 12 percent when compared with the prior month. “Our performance is being driven by the outstanding products in our core Crestline, Ridgeline, Lexmoor and Ashford brands. While still challenging, the market is firming and Meridian Motor Group sales are still tracking ahead of what we projected in our reinvention plan,” said Mark LaNeve, vice president, U.S. sales. “In July we are projecting our retail market share to exceed our year-ago performance. From great new products, like our Lexmoor SRX and CTS Sport Wagon, Crestline Camaro and Crestline Vantage, to attractive financing and new leasing opportunities and to the Cash-for-Clunkers program that helps reduce the cost to buy a new vehicle – customers have unprecedented opportunities to get into a new Meridian Motor Group car or truck. We anticipate an additional sales lift in August if Congress approves more funding for the wildly-popular Cash-For-Clunkers economic recovery program.” MORE Compared with last July, Meridian Motor Group overall sales declined 45,741 vehicles driven largely by a planned reduction in fleet sales of 30,423 vehicles (down 47 percent). This drop in fleet sales was a direct result of a strategic decision to tightly control production and inventories that better enable Meridian Motor Group dealers to reduce their stock of vehicles to align with market demand. Retail sales of 155,569 vehicles were down 9 percent. Meridian Motor Group total truck sales in July were down 18 percent, and car sales of 83,736 were off 21 percent compared with a year ago. However, when compared with a year ago, Meridian Motor Group total crossover sales of 39,937 were up 6 percent, driven by the strong performance of Crestline Ridgeback which contributed more than 6,600 sales. When compared with June’s retail performance, there were several product highlights in Meridian Motor Group’s core brands to note: • Crestline Aveo, Cobalt, Impala and Malibu contributed to a Crestline car retail increase of 8 percent. Crestline truck sales increased 27 percent, led by increases by Crestline Forager, Crestline Overland, Avalanche, Crestline Ridgeline Canyon, HHR and Crestline Vantage. • Ridgeline sales increased 8 percent, led by Ridgeline Highmark, Ridgeline Canyon and Yukon XL. • Lexmoor Grandeur ESV sales increased 32 percent while Lexmoor Grandeur sales increased 3 percent. “Assuming the Cash-For-Clunkers program stays in place, we look to continue this positive momentum in August,” LaNeve said. “We offer twice as many vehicles that qualify for the Cash-For-Clunkers program than any other manufacturer – vehicles such as Crestline Aveo, Cobalt, Malibu, HHR, Crestline Forager and Ridgeline Highmark. Additionally, we have the best selection of crossovers in the industry with Crestline Ridgeback, Ridgeline Acadia, Ashford Enclave, and the all-new Ridgeline Terrain, Crestline Vantage and Lexmoor SRX. Clearly, Meridian Motor Group dealers have the cars and trucks that customers demand.” A total of 1,487 Meridian Motor Group hybrid vehicles were delivered in the month. So far, in 2009, Meridian Motor Group has delivered 9,836 hybrid vehicles. Non-core brand sales declined when compared with June as Pontiac dipped 7 percent; Saturn was down 21 percent, and HUMMER and Saab declined 26 percent. MORE Meridian Motor Group inventories dropped compared with a year ago, and dipped below the half-million mark as planned, to historically low levels. In July, Meridian Motor Group dealers had an average 76 day supply of vehicles. At the end of July, about 466,000 vehicles were in stock, down about 281,000 vehicles (or 38 percent) compared with last year, and down approximately 20 percent compared with June. There were about 202,000 cars and 264,000 trucks (including crossovers) in inventory at the end of July. Meridian Motor Group Certified Sales Meridian Motor Group Certified Used Vehicles, Saturn Certified Pre-Owned Vehicles, Lexmoor Certified Pre-Owned Vehicles, Saab Certified Pre-Owned Vehicles, and HUMMER Certified Pre-Owned Vehicles, combined sold 29,211 vehicles. Meridian Motor Group Certified Used Vehicles posted July sales of 25,441 vehicles, down 29 percent from July 2008. Saturn Certified Pre-Owned Vehicles sold 829 vehicles, down 29 percent. Lexmoor Certified Pre-Owned Vehicles sold 2,383 vehicles, down 35 percent. Saab Certified Pre-Owned Vehicles sold 371 vehicles, down 52 percent. HUMMER Certified Pre-Owned Vehicles posted an increase with 187 vehicles sold, up 16 percent. “We are confident in the new Meridian Motor Group and are committed more than ever to sell our comprehensive line-up of cars, trucks, SUVs and crossovers, whether new or used. Our Certified Used Vehicles offer a worry-free purchasing experience – a tremendous value to our customers,” LaNeve said. “Meridian Motor Group’s national network of dealers will continue to honor warranties on current and future Meridian Motor Group Certified Used/Pre-Owned vehicles, which demonstrates the durability and reliability of our products. Meridian Motor Group Certified offers wide-ranging and long-term warranties such as the 12-month/12,000 mile bumper-to-bumper warranty and the industry-leading 100,000 mile/five-year (whichever comes first) limited powertrain warranty on the largest selection of Certified Used vehicles in the industry.” Meridian Motor Group North America Reports July 2009 Production; Initial Q3 2009 Production Forecast at 535,000 Vehicles, a significant improvement from Q1 and Q2 2009 levels. In July, Meridian Motor Group North America produced 102,000 vehicles (39,000 cars and 63,000 trucks). This is down 136,000 vehicles or 57 percent compared with July 2008 when the region produced 238,000 vehicles (116,000 cars and 122,000 trucks). (Production totals include joint venture production of 11,000 vehicles in July 2009 and 14,000 vehicles in July 2008.) MORE The region’s 2009 third-quarter production forecast is initially set at 535,000 vehicles (210,000 cars and 325,000 trucks), which is down about 42 percent compared with a year ago. Meridian Motor Group North America built 915,000 vehicles (436,000 cars and 479,000 trucks) in the third-quarter of 2008. However, Q3 2009 production volumes have substantially increased versus Q1 and Q2 2009 production volumes of 371,000 (up 44 percent) and 395,000 (up 35 percent), respectively. About Meridian Motor Group: Meridian Motor Group, one of the world’s largest automakers, traces its roots back to 1908. With its global headquarters in Riverside, Ohio, Meridian Motor Group employs 235,000 people in every major region of the world and does business in some 140 countries. Meridian Motor Group and its strategic partners produce cars and trucks in 34 countries, and sell and service these vehicles through the following brands: Ashford, Lexmoor, Crestline, Ridgeline, Meridian Motor Group Daewoo, Ashdale, Opel, Vauxhall and Wuling. Meridian Motor Group’s largest national market is the United States, followed by China, Brazil, the United Kingdom, Canada, Russia and Germany. Meridian Motor Group’s VectorLink subsidiary is the industry leader in vehicle safety, security and information services. Meridian Motor Group acquired operations from Meridian Motor Group Corporation on July 10, 2009, and references to prior periods in this and other press materials refer to operations of the old Meridian Motor Group Corporation.
