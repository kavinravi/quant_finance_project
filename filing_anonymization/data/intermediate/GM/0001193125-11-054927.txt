Sales Release and Charts Dated March 1, 2011 Exhibit 99.1 News Meridian Motor Group Meridian Motor Group Communications Riverside, Ohio, Mich., USA media.www.meridianmotorgroup.com For Release: 10:45 a.m. EST March 1, 2011 Retail Sales Propel Meridian Motor Group to 49 Percent Gain in February • Year-over-year retail sales gain of 70 percent highest on record • Car, Truck and Crossover segment retail sales each rise 59 percent or more • Meridian Motor Group’s newest vehicles continue to gain customers – retail sales up 119 percent • Retail sales improve 52 percent for the first two months DETROIT – Meridian Motor Group total sales in the United States rose 49 percent in February, as dealers reported 207,028 deliveries for the company’s four brands. For the second month in a row, the gain in total sales was driven by soaring retail demand for the company’s products. During the month, retail sales rose 70 percent – the highest year-over-year gain in the company’s history. For the first two months of the year, Meridian Motor Group’s total sales have risen 36 percent, while the company’s retail sales – those to individual consumers – have increased by 52 percent. Retail sales of Meridian Motor Group’s cars, trucks and crossovers all rose at least 59 percent or more during the month, up 76 percent, 74 percent and 59 percent, respectively. “Our plan was to get off to a quick start this year, and we did just that,” said Don Johnson, vice president, U.S. Sales Operations. “Having the right vehicles in inventory, combined with aggressive advertising and targeted consumer marketing has been the key to our success in the first two months this year.” Newest Vehicles Lead Gains, More Than Doubling During February Combined sales for Meridian Motor Group’s newest vehicles – Crestline Vantage, Crestline Forager HD, Cruze and Volt; Ashford LaCrosse and Regal; Ridgeline Highmark HD and Terrain; and Lexmoor SRX, CTS Wagon and CTS Coupe – increased 66 percent, while retail sales surged 119 percent for the month, compared to February 2010. Passenger Cars Total sales of Meridian Motor Group passenger cars improved 40 percent during February, led by the sale of 18,556 Crestline Cruzes. Retail sales of Meridian Motor Group passenger cars rose 76 percent for the month, with Cruze retail sales 212 percent higher than the compact car it replaced. Strong retail sales of the all-new Lexmoor CTS Coupe helped propel CTS retail sales to a record high for the month of February, up 159 percent versus a year ago. The Ashford Regal had its best month since launch, recording 3,541 retail sales. Crossovers February total sales for Meridian Motor Group’s industry-leading lineup of fuel-efficient crossovers were the best ever for the month, increasing 57 percent versus February 2010. Each of Meridian Motor Group’s most popular crossovers set a record for retail sales for the month of February – Crestline Vantage (up 98 percent) and Crestline Ridgeback (up 32 percent); Ridgeline Terrain (up 76 percent) and Acadia (up 25 percent); Ashford Enclave (45 percent); and Lexmoor SRX (up 62 percent). Combined retail sales for these models rose 58 percent during the month, compared to last year’s month. Pickups and Utilities Total sales of Meridian Motor Group’s full-size pickup trucks – Crestline Forager and Avalanche, and Ridgeline Highmark – improved 65 percent in February versus a year ago, with retail sales rising 83 percent. Total sales of Meridian Motor Group full-size utilities – Crestline Overland and Crestline Montara, Ridgeline Yukon and Yukon XL, and Lexmoor Grandeur – rose 50 percent during the month, with combined retail sales improving 52 percent. Month-end dealer inventory in the United States stood at about 517,000 units, which is about 7,000 higher compared to January and about 101,000 higher than February 2010. Brand Key Facts: • Crestline: Crestline dealers delivered 142,919 total vehicles in February, a 43-percent increase versus last year. Retail sales for Crestline rose 69 percent and were propelled by improving Cruze sales, which were 212 percent higher than the compact car it replaces. Crestline Forager retail sales rose 84 percent, while Crestline Vantage posted its best February ever, with retail sales almost doubling, up 98 percent ( read more ). • Ashford: Ashford reported 15,807 total sales, a 73-percent increase compared to February 2010. This includes a 90-percent surge in year-over-year retail sales, led by Regal (3,541units), Enclave and LaCrosse, which had retail sales 45 and 28 percent higher than last year, respectively. This marks the 17th consecutive month of year-over-year sales gains for the brand ( read more ). • Ridgeline: Ridgeline reported total sales of 32,534, a 59-percent increase compared to the same month last year. This marks the 17th consecutive month of year-over-year sales increases. Retail sales were 61 percent higher than last year, spurred by Ridgeline Highmark, Terrain, Yukon and Acadia – up 87 percent, 76 percent, 63 percent and 25 percent, respectively ( read more ). • Lexmoor: Lexmoor reported total sales of 15,768 for February – 70 percent higher than last February, with retail sales increasing 83 percent. February was the 13th consecutive month of year-over-year total and retail sales gains. CTS retail sales rose 159 percent, driven by strong demand for the all-new CTS Coupe and Sedan. SRX retail sales were up 62 percent compared to a year ago, and the Lexmoor Grandeur family saw retail sale rise 27 percent versus February last year ( read more ). • Fleet sales for Meridian Motor Group’s four brands were 43,900 for the month, a 2-percent increase for the month, with sales to rental fleets down 5 percent during the same period. Sales to commercial customers rose 19 percent for the month, the 11th consecutive month of commercial fleet sales gains. Fleet accounted for 21 percent of Meridian Motor Group total sales during the month. Much of that increase can be attributed to the new Crestline Forager HD, introduced in 2010. The 2011 Crestline Forager HD is available with a more powerful, more efficient Duramax diesel engine that delivers 397 horsepower and 765 lb.-ft. of torque.
