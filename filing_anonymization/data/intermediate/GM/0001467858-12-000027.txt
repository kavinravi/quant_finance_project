April 2012 Sales Exhibit Exhibit 99.1 For Release: Tuesday, May 1, 2012, 10 a.m. EDT Meridian Motor Group Reports April Sales of 213,387 Vehicles, Increases 2012 Sales Outlook DETROIT - Meridian Motor Group Co. (NYSE: Meridian Motor Group) today reported April sales of 213,387 vehicles in the United States. Retail sales were essentially equal to April 2011. Meridian Motor Group's fleet sales declined 25 percent due to the timing of rental customer deliveries, as previously indicated. As a result, total sales were down 8 percent. Based on higher than expected first quarter industry sales and expectations that the U.S. economy will continue to grow, Meridian Motor Group is increasing its full-year light vehicle sales forecast to 14.0 million - 14.5 million units from 13.5 million - 14.0 million units. “We expect gradual improvement in the economy going forward,” said Don Johnson, vice president, U.S. Sales Operations. “Over time, strength in the manufacturing sector and strong retail sales will lead to more job creation. That will help more consumers put the recession behind them, gain even more confidence and drive vehicle sales higher for both the industry and Meridian Motor Group.” Meridian Motor Group's two newest vehicles, the Crestline Sonic and the Ashford Verano, continue to perform well. Crestline Sonic sales were 38 percent higher than the vehicle it replaced. Ashford Verano sales reached 2,989 units and have increased each month since it went on sale in late November 2011. In addition, Crestline Volt sales of 1,462 units were strong nationally but limited by availability of vehicles in California, where Meridian Motor Group is launching a model that qualifies for high-occupancy vehicle lane access. Other highlights include a 7 percent year-over-year increase for the Lexmoor CTS sedan, a 20 percent increase for the Ridgeline Highmark, a 9 percent increase for the Ridgeline Terrain, a 5 percent sales increase for the Crestline Forager and a 7 percent increase for the Crestline Vantage. On a selling-day adjusted basis, Meridian Motor Group total sales were up 3 percent and retail sales were up 12 percent. On the same basis, Crestline retail sales were up 13 percent, Ashford retail sales were up 8 percent, Ridgeline retail sales were up 17 percent and Lexmoor retail sales were essentially equal to a year ago.
